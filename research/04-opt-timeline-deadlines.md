# OPT Timeline and Deadlines for F-1 Students

> Comprehensive reference covering application windows, processing times, grace periods, STEM OPT extensions, cap-gap rules, and critical deadlines.
>
> Last updated: April 2026

---

## 1. OPT Application Timeline

### Filing Window for Post-Completion OPT

F-1 students applying for post-completion OPT must file Form I-765 (Application for Employment Authorization) within a **150-day filing window**:

| Boundary | Rule |
|----------|------|
| **Earliest** | 90 days **before** your program end date |
| **Latest** | 60 days **after** your program end date |

Within this 150-day window, there is an additional constraint:

- You must file Form I-765 **within 30 days** of the date your DSO (Designated School Official) enters the OPT recommendation into SEVIS.

This means you need to coordinate with your DSO. If your DSO enters the recommendation too early or too late relative to your own readiness, you could miss the 30-day sub-deadline even if you are within the 150-day window.

### Step-by-Step Filing Sequence

1. **Meet with your DSO** to request an OPT recommendation (ideally 3-4 months before graduation).
2. **DSO enters recommendation** into SEVIS and issues an updated I-20 with the OPT recommendation.
3. **You file Form I-765** with USCIS within 30 days of the DSO recommendation, and within the 90-before / 60-after window.
4. **USCIS processes** the application and, if approved, mails your EAD card.

> **SEVIS auto-cancellation:** Any OPT request that remains in "Requested" status for longer than 180 days is automatically canceled by SEVIS.

## 2. EAD Card Processing Times

### Current Averages (2025-2026)

| Filing Method | Typical Processing Time |
|---------------|------------------------|
| **Online filing** | 2-3 months |
| **Paper filing** | 3-5 months |
| **Premium processing** | 30 business days (fee: $1,685) |

- Most OPT EAD applications are adjudicated within **90-120 days** (approximately 3-4 months).
- Online-filed applications are routinely approved faster than paper submissions.
- After approval, the physical EAD card is typically produced and mailed within **2 weeks**.
- Premium processing guarantees a *decision* within 30 business days but does **not** expedite the physical card production or shipment.

### Practical Implication

Because processing can take 3-5 months, students who wait until after graduation to file risk not receiving their EAD before their requested OPT start date. **Filing early (up to 90 days before program end) is strongly recommended.**

If your application has been pending for more than 90 days without a decision, you may contact USCIS or submit a case inquiry through the USCIS Contact Center.

## 3. OPT Start Date Rules

### Post-Completion OPT

When you apply for post-completion OPT, you request a specific start date on your I-765. The rules are:

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Earliest start date** | The day after your program end date |
| **Latest start date** | 60 days after your program end date |

- You **cannot work** until you have your EAD card in hand **and** the start date on the EAD has arrived.
- If USCIS has not yet approved your application by your requested start date, you must wait for the EAD card -- you cannot begin working.
- Your unemployment clock starts on the OPT start date printed on your EAD, regardless of whether you have found a job.

### Pre-Completion OPT

Pre-completion OPT must begin and end **before** the program end date. It reduces your available post-completion OPT time on a one-for-one basis (e.g., 4 months of pre-completion OPT leaves only 8 months of post-completion OPT).

## 4. The 12-Month OPT Period

### Duration and Dates

- Standard post-completion OPT is authorized for a **maximum of 12 months**.
- The start date is the date you requested on your I-765 (within the rules above).
- The end date is exactly **12 months** after the start date (minus any pre-completion OPT time used).
- Example: OPT start date of July 1, 2026 means OPT ends June 30, 2027.

### Unemployment Limits During OPT

| OPT Type | Maximum Aggregate Unemployment |
|----------|-------------------------------|
| **Standard 12-month OPT** | 90 days |
| **STEM OPT (full 36 months)** | 150 days total (90 initial + 60 extension) |

- Unemployment days are counted in **aggregate** (not consecutive) and include weekends and holidays.
- The clock starts on your EAD start date.
- Exceeding the unemployment limit is a **violation of F-1 status** and can result in SEVIS record termination.

### What Counts as Employment

To stop the unemployment clock, you must have a job that:
- Is directly related to your field of study
- Is at least 20 hours per week
- Is paid (volunteering does not count for standard OPT; unpaid work does not stop the clock)

## 5. Grace Periods

### 60-Day Post-OPT Grace Period

After your OPT EAD expires (the end date on your card), you receive a **60-day grace period**:

| What you CAN do | What you CANNOT do |
|------------------|--------------------|
| Remain in the U.S. legally | Work (employment authorization has ended) |
| Prepare to depart the U.S. | Start a new job |
| Transfer to another school/program | Extend OPT (unless filing STEM extension) |
| Apply for change of status | Travel and re-enter on F-1 (risky) |

### 60-Day Post-Program-Completion Grace Period

Separately, after your program end date (before OPT starts), you have a **60-day grace period** to either:
- Depart the U.S.
- Apply for OPT (if within the filing window)
- Transfer to another school
- Change to another visa status

### Key Distinction

These are two different 60-day periods:
1. **Post-program completion** (60 days after your program end date) -- this is your deadline to *file* for OPT.
2. **Post-OPT** (60 days after your EAD expires) -- this is your deadline to leave the U.S. or change status after OPT ends.

## 6. STEM OPT Extension

### Eligibility Requirements

- You hold a bachelor's, master's, or doctoral degree in a STEM-designated field (from the DHS STEM Designated Degree Program List).
- You are currently on post-completion OPT with a valid EAD.
- Your employer is enrolled in **E-Verify**.
- You and your employer have completed **Form I-983** (Training Plan for STEM OPT Students).

### Filing Window

| Boundary | Rule |
|----------|------|
| **Earliest** | 90 days before your current OPT EAD expiration date |
| **Latest** | Your current OPT EAD expiration date (USCIS must *receive* the application by this date) |
| **Additional constraint** | Within 60 days of the DSO entering the STEM OPT recommendation into SEVIS |

If your current OPT expires while your STEM extension application is pending, you receive an **automatic 180-day extension** of employment authorization while USCIS adjudicates your application, provided you filed on time.

### STEM OPT Duration

- 24 additional months (for a total of up to 36 months of OPT).
- You may apply for a second STEM OPT extension if you earn another qualifying STEM degree at a higher education level.

### Reporting Requirements During STEM OPT

| Requirement | Deadline |
|-------------|----------|
| Validate your U.S. address | Every 6 months via the SEVP Portal |
| Report name or address changes | Within 10 days |
| Report employer changes | Within 10 days (new I-983 required) |
| Submit 12-month evaluation (I-983) | Within 10 days of the 12-month mark |
| Submit final evaluation (I-983) | Within 10 days of STEM OPT end date |

## 7. Cap-Gap Extension

### What Is Cap-Gap?

The "cap-gap" is the period between the expiration of an F-1 student's OPT authorization and the start of H-1B employment (typically October 1). If you are selected in the H-1B lottery and your employer files a timely cap-subject H-1B petition, your F-1 status and work authorization are automatically extended.

### 2025 Rule Change (Effective January 17, 2025)

| Before 2025 Rule | After 2025 Rule |
|-------------------|-----------------|
| Cap-gap extended F-1 status and work authorization until **October 1** | Cap-gap now extends until **April 1** of the relevant fiscal year |

This change applies starting with the FY 2026 H-1B registration period and forward.

### Eligibility for Cap-Gap

All of the following must be true:
1. Your employer filed a **timely H-1B cap-subject petition** with USCIS.
2. You were maintaining **valid F-1 status** when the petition was filed.
3. You were in an **authorized period of post-completion OPT** (including STEM OPT) when the petition was filed.
4. USCIS **received** the petition and issued a receipt notice.

### Cap-Gap and STEM OPT Interaction

- You may apply for a STEM OPT extension **during** the cap-gap period.
- You may **not** apply for STEM OPT after the cap-gap terminates (e.g., if the H-1B petition is denied, withdrawn, or revoked) and you have entered the 60-day grace period.

## 8. Key Milestones and Deadlines to Track

### Master Deadline Calendar

| # | Milestone | Deadline | Consequence of Missing |
|---|-----------|----------|----------------------|
| 1 | Request OPT recommendation from DSO | 3-4 months before graduation (recommended) | Risk missing the filing window |
| 2 | File I-765 after DSO recommendation | Within 30 days of DSO entering recommendation in SEVIS | Application rejected |
| 3 | File I-765 for post-completion OPT | No earlier than 90 days before, no later than 60 days after program end | Cannot apply for OPT |
| 4 | OPT start date on EAD | No later than 60 days after program end | Must select valid date |
| 5 | Begin employment on OPT | By OPT start date on EAD (to avoid unemployment days) | Unemployment clock is ticking |
| 6 | Aggregate unemployment limit | 90 days (standard) / 150 days (STEM) | SEVIS termination |
| 7 | STEM OPT extension filing | Up to 90 days before OPT expiration; USCIS must receive by OPT end date | Lose eligibility; OPT ends |
| 8 | I-983 Training Plan submission | Before filing STEM OPT I-765 | Cannot file STEM extension |
| 9 | I-983 12-month evaluation | Within 10 days of the 12-month mark | Compliance violation |
| 10 | SEVP Portal address validation | Every 6 months during STEM OPT | Compliance violation |
| 11 | Report employer changes | Within 10 days | Compliance violation |
| 12 | 60-day grace period after OPT | Depart, transfer, or change status within 60 days | Out of status / unlawful presence |

## 9. What Happens If You Miss a Deadline

### Missed I-765 Filing Window (90 before / 60 after)

- You **lose eligibility** for post-completion OPT entirely for that degree level.
- You must depart the U.S. within your grace period or change to another visa status.
- There is **no late filing exception**.

### Missed the 30-Day DSO Recommendation Window

- Your I-765 will be rejected by USCIS.
- You must get a new DSO recommendation and refile, but only if you are still within the 150-day window.

### Exceeded Unemployment Limit

- Your DSO is required to report this to SEVP.
- Your SEVIS record may be **terminated**, ending your F-1 status.
- You must depart the U.S. or apply for reinstatement (difficult and not guaranteed).

### Missed STEM OPT Extension Deadline

- If USCIS does not receive your I-765 by your OPT expiration date, you **cannot** apply for the STEM extension.
- Your OPT ends, and you enter the 60-day grace period.

### Overstayed Grace Period

- Remaining beyond the 60-day grace period constitutes **unlawful presence**.
- Accruing unlawful presence can trigger **3-year or 10-year bars** to re-entry:
  - 180 days to 1 year of unlawful presence: 3-year bar
  - More than 1 year: 10-year bar

## 10. Practical Timeline Examples

### Scenario A: Spring Graduate (May 2026)

| Date | Action |
|------|--------|
| **Feb 15, 2026** | Meet with DSO, request OPT recommendation (90 days before May 16 end date) |
| **Feb 20, 2026** | DSO enters recommendation in SEVIS, issues new I-20 |
| **Mar 1, 2026** | File I-765 online (within 30 days of DSO recommendation) |
| **May 16, 2026** | Program end date (graduation) |
| **June 1, 2026** | Requested OPT start date (within 60 days of program end) |
| **Jun-Jul 2026** | Receive EAD card (approx. 90-120 days after filing) |
| **May 31, 2027** | OPT end date (12 months from start) |
| **Jul 30, 2027** | 60-day grace period ends; must depart or change status |

### Scenario B: STEM Graduate Applying for Extension

| Date | Action |
|------|--------|
| **Jun 1, 2026** | Post-completion OPT starts |
| **May 31, 2027** | Post-completion OPT expires |
| **Mar 2, 2027** | Earliest STEM OPT filing date (90 days before expiration) |
| **Mar 2027** | Complete I-983 Training Plan with employer |
| **Mar 15, 2027** | DSO enters STEM recommendation; you file I-765 |
| **May 31, 2027** | OPT expires, but 180-day auto-extension kicks in (pending STEM application) |
| **Jun 1, 2027** | STEM OPT period begins (if approved) |
| **May 31, 2029** | STEM OPT expires (24 additional months) |
| **Jul 30, 2029** | 60-day grace period ends |

### Scenario C: H-1B Cap-Gap (FY 2027)

| Date | Action |
|------|--------|
| **Jun 1, 2026** | OPT starts |
| **Mar 2027** | Employer registers you for H-1B lottery (FY 2028) |
| **Mar-Apr 2027** | Selected in H-1B lottery; employer files H-1B petition |
| **May 31, 2027** | OPT EAD expires |
| **Jun 1, 2027** | Cap-gap auto-extension begins (F-1 status + work authorization continue) |
| **Oct 1, 2027** | H-1B employment start date (if approved by then) |
| **Apr 1, 2028** | Cap-gap extension end date (if H-1B is still pending under new rule) |

> Under the pre-2025 rule, cap-gap would have ended Oct 1. The new rule extends protection to April 1.

| Number | What It Means |
|--------|---------------|
| **90 days** | Earliest you can file I-765 before program end |
| **60 days** | Latest you can file I-765 after program end |
| **30 days** | Window to file I-765 after DSO enters recommendation |
| **150 days** | Total filing window (90 + 60) |
| **90 days** | Max aggregate unemployment on standard OPT |
| **150 days** | Max aggregate unemployment on STEM OPT (36-month total) |
| **60 days** | Grace period after OPT ends |
| **180 days** | Auto-extension of work authorization while STEM OPT application is pending |
| **90 days** | Earliest you can file STEM OPT extension before OPT expiration |
