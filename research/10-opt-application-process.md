# OPT Application Process & EAD Card Details

Research report covering the full OPT (Optional Practical Training) application lifecycle: from filing Form I-765 through receiving the Employment Authorization Document (EAD) card, including expedited processing, common pitfalls, and the SEVP Portal.

---

## 1. Step-by-Step OPT Application Process

### Pre-Filing

1. **Complete one full academic year** of full-time study in valid F-1 status.
2. **Identify your OPT type**: pre-completion (before graduation) or post-completion (after graduation).
3. **Meet with your DSO** (Designated School Official) at your school's international student office to discuss eligibility and timing.

### Filing Steps

| Step | Action | Details |
|------|--------|---------|
| 1 | Request DSO recommendation | Your DSO endorses your Form I-20 and makes the OPT notation in SEVIS |
| 2 | Receive endorsed I-20 | DSO issues a new I-20 with the OPT recommendation on page 2 |
| 3 | Create myUSCIS account | Go to my.uscis.gov to create an online account (recommended for faster processing) |
| 4 | Complete Form I-765 | Fill out the Application for Employment Authorization, selecting the correct category code |
| 5 | Gather supporting documents | Assemble all required evidence (see Section 3 below) |
| 6 | Pay filing fee | $410 for I-765 (as of 2026) |
| 7 | Submit application | File online via myUSCIS or mail paper application to the appropriate USCIS lockbox |
| 8 | Receive receipt notice (I-797C) | Arrives within 2-4 weeks confirming USCIS accepted your application |
| 9 | Wait for adjudication | USCIS reviews your application (2-5 months typical) |
| 10 | Receive approval notice + EAD card | EAD card (Form I-766) arrives by mail after approval |

### Critical Filing Window (Post-Completion OPT)

- **Earliest**: 90 days before program end date
- **Latest**: 60 days after program end date
- **DSO I-20 endorsement**: Must be signed within 30 days before filing I-765

Source: [USCIS - Optional Practical Training for F-1 Students](https://www.uscis.gov/working-in-the-united-states/students-and-exchange-visitors/optional-practical-training-opt-for-f-1-students)

## 2. Required Forms

### Form I-765 (Application for Employment Authorization)

The primary application form. Key fields to complete correctly:

- **Eligibility Category**: Select `(c)(3)(A)` for pre-completion OPT or `(c)(3)(B)` for post-completion OPT
- **SEVIS ID**: Your 11-digit SEVIS number from Form I-20
- **Employer information**: Not required at time of filing for post-completion OPT
- **Degree information**: Must match what is recorded in SEVIS

### Form I-20 (Certificate of Eligibility)

- Must contain the DSO's OPT recommendation on page 2
- DSO signature must be dated within **30 days** before you file I-765
- All pages of the I-20 should be submitted

### Form I-907 (Optional -- Premium Processing)

- Filed concurrently with or after I-765
- Requests 30-business-day adjudication
- Separate fee required (see Section 4)

Source: [USCIS - Form I-765](https://www.uscis.gov/i-765)

## 3. Supporting Documents Checklist

| Document | Notes |
|----------|-------|
| Form I-765 (completed and signed) | Online or paper version |
| Form I-20 with OPT recommendation | Signed by DSO within 30 days of filing |
| Passport biographical page copy | Must be valid; at least 6 months remaining recommended |
| Form I-94 (Arrival/Departure Record) | Print from [i94.cbp.dhs.gov](https://i94.cbp.dhs.gov) |
| Passport-style photo | 2x2 inches, color, white background, taken within 30 days (paper filing: 2 physical photos; online: digital upload) |
| Previous EAD cards (if any) | Copies of any prior Employment Authorization Documents |
| All prior I-20s | Including any previously used SEVIS numbers |
| Filing fee payment | $410 -- check/money order (paper) or credit/debit/bank transfer (online) |
| Prior CPT/OPT history | List all previously authorized CPT or OPT with academic levels |

**For STEM OPT Extension (additional documents):**

- Form I-983 (Training Plan) signed by employer
- Evidence of qualifying STEM degree
- Proof employer is E-Verify enrolled
- Copy of current EAD card being extended

## 5. Online vs. Paper Filing

| Factor | Online (myUSCIS) | Paper (Mail) |
|--------|-------------------|--------------|
| **Processing speed** | 2-3 months average | 3-5 months average |
| **Receipt notice** | Instant electronic receipt + paper copy mailed later | Paper receipt mailed in 2-4 weeks |
| **Payment** | Credit/debit card or bank transfer | Check or money order only |
| **Document submission** | Upload digital scans | Mail physical copies |
| **Case tracking** | Real-time via myUSCIS dashboard | Via USCIS case status website |
| **Save & resume** | Yes, but must complete within 30 days or data is erased | N/A |
| **Premium processing** | Can add I-907 online | Must mail separate I-907 form |
| **Recommended?** | Yes -- strongly recommended | Only if online filing is unavailable |

**How to file online:**
1. Go to [my.uscis.gov](https://my.uscis.gov) and create an account
2. Select "File a form online" then choose I-765
3. Complete each section, upload documents
4. Pay the filing fee electronically
5. Submit -- receive electronic receipt number immediately

Source: [USCIS - Forms Available to File Online](https://www.uscis.gov/file-online/forms-available-to-file-online) | [USCIS - F-1 Students Can File I-765 Online](https://www.uscis.gov/archive/f-1-students-seeking-optional-practical-training-can-now-file-form-i-765-online)

## 7. The EAD Card (Form I-766)

### Physical Description

The EAD is a credit-card-sized plastic identification card redesigned in January 2023. Current security features include:

- Updated artwork of the Statue of Liberty
- Holographic images on front and back
- Tactile printing integrated with artwork
- Enhanced optically variable ink
- Layer-reveal feature with partial window on back photo box
- Photo of the cardholder on both front and back

### Information Fields on the EAD Card

**Front of card:**

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Surname / Given Name | Full legal name |
| Category | `C03A` (pre-completion OPT) or `C03B` (post-completion OPT) or `C03C` (STEM OPT) |
| USCIS Number | Your A-Number (Alien Registration Number) |
| Card Number | Unique card identifier |
| Date of Birth | MM/DD/YYYY format |
| Country of Birth | Country name |
| Card Expires | Expiration date of the work authorization |
| Terms and Conditions | e.g., "Student: Post Completion OPT" |
| Photo | Passport-style photo of the cardholder |

**Back of card:**

- Second photo of cardholder
- Machine-readable zone (MRZ)
- "NOT VALID FOR REENTRY TO U.S." annotation (the EAD is not a travel document)

### Important Notes

- The EAD is a **List A document** for Form I-9 purposes (establishes both identity and employment authorization)
- Both old-design and new-design cards remain valid until their printed expiration date
- The EAD is **not** a visa or travel document -- you cannot use it to enter the U.S.

Source: [USCIS - Employment Authorization Document](https://www.uscis.gov/green-card/green-card-processes-and-procedures/employment-authorization-document) | [USCIS - EAD Redesign Comparison (PDF)](https://www.uscis.gov/sites/default/files/document/guides/EAD_Comparison_EN.pdf)

## 8. Receipt Notice vs. Approval Notice vs. EAD Card

| Document | Form Number | What It Means | Can You Work With It? |
|----------|-------------|---------------|----------------------|
| **Receipt Notice** | I-797C | USCIS received and accepted your application | No |
| **Approval Notice** | I-797 | USCIS approved your application | No -- explicitly states "not evidence of employment authorization" |
| **EAD Card** | I-766 | Your physical work authorization card | Yes -- this is your proof of employment eligibility |

**Timeline of arrival:**
1. **Receipt Notice (I-797C)**: 2-4 weeks after filing (instant if filed online)
2. **Approval Notice (I-797)**: When USCIS makes a decision (2-5 months)
3. **EAD Card (I-766)**: Arrives within days to 2 weeks after the approval notice

**Key distinction**: The approval notice and EAD card are separate documents. You may receive the approval notice first but must wait for the physical EAD card before starting work.

Source: [Emory ISSS - OPT While Pending](https://isss.emory.edu/students/opt-pending-uscis-approval-while-opt-beyond) | [Harvard HIO - EAD Issues](https://www.hio.harvard.edu/EAD-issues)

## 9. What to Do While Waiting for EAD

### You CAN:
- **Track your case status** using your receipt number at egov.uscis.gov
- **Sign up for case status alerts** (email and text notifications)
- **Interview and accept job offers** (you just cannot start working yet)
- **Remain in the U.S.** in valid F-1 status
- **Contact USCIS** if processing exceeds posted times (submit an e-Request or call 1-800-375-5283)
- **Request premium processing** by filing Form I-907 even after initial submission

### You CANNOT:
- **Begin employment** before receiving the physical EAD card
- **Use the receipt notice or approval notice** as proof of work authorization

### Strongly Advised Against:
- **International travel** while the application is pending -- re-entry is risky and could result in delays or denial
- **Changing your address** without updating USCIS (use AR-11 online) and your DSO

### If Your Application Has Been Pending Over 90 Days:
1. Check posted processing times at egov.uscis.gov
2. If outside normal processing times, submit an **e-Request** (case inquiry) via the USCIS website
3. Contact your school's international office for guidance
4. Consider filing for premium processing (I-907)

Source: [Illinois State ISSS - While Application is Pending](https://isss.illinoisstate.edu/employment/f-1-optional-practical-training/pending-application/) | [Stanford Bechtel - OPT Pending More Than 90 Days](https://bechtel.stanford.edu/navigate-international-life/employment/f-1-student-employment/optional-practical-training-opt/opt)

## 10. Premium Processing (Expedited)

### Overview

- **Form**: I-907, Request for Premium Processing Service
- **Fee**: $1,780 (effective March 1, 2026; was $1,685 before)
- **Guarantee**: USCIS will take action within **30 business days** (approximately 6 calendar weeks)
- **Available for**: Initial OPT (c)(3)(B) and STEM OPT extension (c)(3)(C)

### "Action" means one of:
- Approval
- Denial
- Request for Evidence (RFE)
- Notice of Intent to Deny (NOID)

If USCIS issues an RFE, the 30-day clock resets after you respond.

### How to Request

- **With initial filing**: Submit I-907 together with I-765 (online or paper)
- **After filing**: You can add premium processing to a pending I-765 at any time
- **Online**: File through myUSCIS
- **Paper**: Mail to the designated lockbox address

### Important Limitation

Premium processing guarantees a **decision** within 30 business days. It does **not** expedite the physical production and mailing of the EAD card, which still takes approximately 2 weeks after approval.

Source: [USCIS - Form I-907](https://www.uscis.gov/i-907) | [USCIS - Premium Processing Announcement for OPT](https://www.uscis.gov/newsroom/news-releases/uscis-announces-premium-processing-new-online-filing-procedures-for-certain-f-1-students-seeking-opt)

## 11. Common RFE (Request for Evidence) Reasons

| RFE Reason | How to Avoid |
|------------|-------------|
| **Expired or soon-expiring passport** | Ensure passport is valid for at least 6 months beyond your OPT end date |
| **Incorrect eligibility category code** | Double-check: `(c)(3)(A)` pre-completion, `(c)(3)(B)` post-completion, `(c)(3)(C)` STEM extension |
| **Missing or illegible documents** | Upload clear, complete scans; include all pages of I-20 |
| **Photo does not meet specifications** | Follow U.S. Department of State photo guidelines exactly |
| **SEVIS number mismatch** | Verify your SEVIS ID matches across all documents |
| **Unsigned or incorrectly signed check** | Double-check payment; online filing avoids this issue |
| **E-Verify number mismatch** (STEM) | Confirm the employer's E-Verify number and legal company name |
| **I-20 endorsement older than 30 days** | Time your DSO endorsement close to your filing date |
| **Missing prior OPT/CPT history** | List all prior practical training in Part 6 of I-765 |

Source: [SJSU ISSS - Rejections, Denials, and RFEs for OPT (PDF)](https://www.sjsu.edu/isss/docs/rejections-denials-rfes.pdf) | [TechFetch - Top 12 Reasons for STEM OPT RFE](https://h1b.techfetch.com/know-the-reasons-for-stem-opt-rfe/)

## 12. Denial Reasons and How to Avoid Them

### Top Denial Reasons

| Denial Reason | Prevention |
|---------------|------------|
| **Filed more than 60 days after program end date** | Track your program end date carefully; file within the 90-day-before to 60-day-after window |
| **Already received OPT at the same degree level** | OPT is authorized once per degree level; a new degree level is needed for new OPT |
| **Less than one academic year of full-time study** | Complete at least one full academic year before applying |
| **Filed after 60-day grace period expired** | If you've graduated and the 60-day grace period has passed, you are no longer eligible |
| **I-20 endorsement missing or invalid** | Verify your DSO properly recommended OPT in SEVIS and signed the I-20 |
| **Unauthorized employment** | Any unauthorized work (including exceeding CPT limits) can result in denial |
| **Out-of-status issues** | Maintain continuous F-1 status; address any gaps before applying |

### How to Minimize Risk

1. **Start early**: Begin the process with your DSO at least 90 days before graduation
2. **File online**: Faster processing and immediate receipt confirmation reduces timing risks
3. **Triple-check the form**: Review every field, especially eligibility category, SEVIS number, and personal details
4. **Keep copies**: Retain copies of everything you submit
5. **Respond to RFEs promptly**: If you receive an RFE, respond well before the deadline with complete documentation

Source: [SJSU ISSS - Rejections, Denials, and RFEs for OPT (PDF)](https://www.sjsu.edu/isss/docs/rejections-denials-rfes.pdf)

## 13. SEVP Portal for OPT Students

### What Is It?

The SEVP (Student and Exchange Visitor Program) Portal is an online tool at [sevp.ice.gov/opt](https://sevp.ice.gov/opt) that allows F-1 students on post-completion OPT to directly report changes to SEVP without going through their DSO for every update.

### Who Has Access?

- F-1 students on **post-completion OPT** (including STEM OPT extension)
- M-1 students on practical training
- Access is activated after the DSO recommends OPT in SEVIS

### Key Features

| Feature | What You Can Do |
|---------|-----------------|
| **Update physical address** | Change your U.S. residential address (system validates the address) |
| **Update mailing address** | Change where you receive mail |
| **Update phone number** | Update contact telephone number |
| **Add employer** (post-completion OPT) | Report new employer name, address, start date |
| **Edit employer** | Update existing employer details |
| **Delete employer** | Remove an employer if you leave a job |
| **View OPT status** | See your current OPT authorization dates and status |

### STEM OPT Limitations

Students on STEM OPT can update personal contact info and edit existing employer info in the portal, but **new employer information must be submitted to the DSO via Form I-983** -- it cannot be added directly through the portal.

### Reporting Requirements

| Requirement | Deadline |
|-------------|----------|
| Report address change | Within **10 days** |
| Report employer change | Within **10 days** |
| Report loss of employment | Within **10 days** |
| STEM OPT 6-month validation | Every **6 months** with DSO |
| Anything not reportable via portal | Report to DSO within **10 days** |

### Unemployment Limits

- **Post-completion OPT**: Maximum 90 days of unemployment during the 12-month OPT period
- **STEM OPT extension**: Additional 60 days (150 days total across the full OPT + STEM period)
- Exceeding the limit results in loss of F-1 status

Source: [Study in the States - SEVP Portal Overview](https://studyinthestates.dhs.gov/sevp-portal-help/getting-started/sevp-portal-overview) | [Study in the States - OPT Reporting Requirements](https://studyinthestates.dhs.gov/sevp-portal-help/maintain-information/opt-student-reporting-requirements) | [ICE - Practical Training](https://www.ice.gov/sevis/practical-training)
