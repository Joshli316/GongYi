# F-1 Cap-Gap Extension Rules: OPT to H-1B Transition

> Comprehensive reference for F-1 students whose employers are filing cap-subject H-1B petitions, covering eligibility, timelines, work authorization, travel, and common pitfalls. Updated to reflect the January 17, 2025 DHS final rule extending cap-gap protections through April 1.

---

## 1. What Is the Cap-Gap?

The **cap-gap** is the period of time between:

- The **end** of an F-1 student's authorized status (post-completion OPT, STEM OPT, or 60-day grace period), and
- The **start** of H-1B employment (typically October 1).

The "cap" refers to the annual numerical limit on H-1B visas. The "gap" refers to the timing mismatch -- many F-1 students' OPT authorization expires before October 1, leaving them in a gap where they would otherwise lose both status and work permission.

**Purpose:** Cap-gap regulations (codified at **8 CFR 214.2(f)(5)(vi)(A)**) automatically extend both F-1 status and, in many cases, OPT employment authorization so that eligible students do not fall out of status while waiting for their H-1B to take effect.

**Regulatory history:**
- **2008:** DHS created the original cap-gap extension (through October 1).
- **January 17, 2025:** DHS final rule extended cap-gap protections from October 1 to **April 1** of the relevant fiscal year, effective starting with FY 2026 H-1B registrations.

---

## 2. Who Qualifies for Cap-Gap Protection

To receive a cap-gap extension, **all** of the following must be true:

1. **You are in valid F-1 status** on the date your employer files the H-1B petition (including during the 60-day grace period after OPT ends).
2. **Your employer files a cap-subject H-1B petition** (Form I-129) requesting a **change of status** (COS) to H-1B -- not consular processing.
3. **The petition is timely filed** during the applicable H-1B filing period (which begins after lottery selection notification).
4. **USCIS receives and receipts the petition** before your F-1 authorized stay expires.
5. **The requested H-1B employment start date** falls between October 1 and April 1 of the relevant fiscal year.

### Who Does NOT Qualify

| Situation | Cap-Gap Eligible? |
|---|---|
| Employer files for consular processing (not COS) | No |
| Student is in pre-completion OPT | No |
| Student's F-1 status has already terminated (violation) | No |
| H-1B petition is cap-exempt (university, nonprofit research) | No (not needed -- no cap) |
| Student was not selected in the H-1B lottery | No |
| Student is selected in the lottery but employer has not yet filed I-129 | No -- lottery selection alone does not trigger cap-gap |

## 3. Automatic Extension of F-1 Status and EAD

The cap-gap extension is **automatic** -- the student does not file a separate application and does not receive a new EAD card.

### What Gets Extended

| Component | Extended? | Details |
|---|---|---|
| **F-1 status** | Yes | Extended for all eligible students, even if OPT has ended and the student is in the 60-day grace period |
| **OPT/STEM OPT work authorization** | Yes, if OPT was active when I-129 was filed | Only if the student had valid post-completion OPT or STEM OPT employment authorization on the date the H-1B petition was filed |
| **Work authorization during grace period** | No | If the student's OPT already expired and the petition was filed during the 60-day grace period, F-1 status is extended but work authorization is **not** |

### How It Works in Practice

1. Employer files Form I-129 with USCIS requesting COS to H-1B.
2. USCIS receipts the petition and sends data to SEVIS.
3. SEVIS automatically updates the student's record to show cap-gap extension.
4. If SEVIS does not auto-update, the DSO can request a data fix through the SEVP Response Center (SRC).
5. The student continues in F-1 status (and continues working, if OPT was active) until the cap-gap end date.

### Proof of Status and Work Authorization

During the cap-gap period, employers can accept the following for I-9 purposes:

- The **expired EAD** (Form I-766) with the appropriate category code (C03A for OPT, C03C for STEM OPT)
- Combined with evidence that the H-1B petition is pending (I-797C receipt notice)
- USCIS provides an automatic extension of EAD validity for I-9/E-Verify purposes

## 4. Cap-Gap Timeline

### Key Dates for FY 2027 (Typical Cycle)

| Date | Event |
|---|---|
| **March (registration period)** | Employer submits H-1B electronic registration for the lottery |
| **Late March / early April** | USCIS notifies selected registrations |
| **April 1 onward** | H-1B filing window opens; employer files Form I-129 with COS request |
| **June 30** | Common OPT end date for May graduates (12 months after degree conferral) |
| **Up to April 1 of next year** | Cap-gap extends F-1 status and work authorization (under 2025 final rule) |
| **October 1** | Standard H-1B start date; if approved, student transitions to H-1B status |

### Cap-Gap Duration Under the 2025 Final Rule

**Before the rule (pre-January 2025):** Cap-gap extended status only through **October 1**.

**After the rule (effective FY 2026 onward):** Cap-gap extends status and work authorization through **April 1** of the following year, or until the H-1B petition is approved/denied/withdrawn, whichever comes first.

This change is significant because H-1B adjudications often extend well past October 1 due to Requests for Evidence (RFEs) and processing backlogs. Students previously lost status and work authorization on October 1 if their petition was still pending.

## 5. What Happens Based on H-1B Petition Outcome

| Outcome | Status Extension | Work Authorization | What to Do |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Selected in lottery, I-129 filed and receipted** | Extended through April 1 (or approval date) | Extended if OPT was active at filing | Continue working; monitor case status |
| **H-1B approved with COS** | F-1 ends; H-1B begins on start date (usually Oct. 1) | H-1B work authorization begins | Transition complete; F-1 record completes in SEVIS |
| **H-1B approved but COS denied** | Cap-gap terminates | Work authorization ends | 60-day grace period to depart or pursue other options |
| **H-1B petition denied** | Cap-gap terminates immediately | Work authorization ends immediately | 60-day grace period from denial notification date |
| **H-1B petition withdrawn by employer** | Cap-gap terminates immediately | Work authorization ends immediately | 60-day grace period from withdrawal date |
| **H-1B petition revoked** | Cap-gap terminates immediately | Work authorization ends immediately | 60-day grace period from revocation date |
| **H-1B petition rejected (not receipted)** | No cap-gap ever started | No extension | Must rely on remaining OPT or grace period |
| **Not selected in lottery** | No cap-gap | No extension | Continue on remaining OPT/STEM OPT or explore other options |

### Critical Exception: Fraud or Misrepresentation

If the COS request is denied due to a **status violation, misrepresentation, or fraud**, the student does **not** receive the 60-day grace period and must depart the United States immediately.

## 6. Cap-Gap and OPT/STEM OPT Interaction

### Standard Post-Completion OPT (12 months)

- Cap-gap extends both F-1 status and OPT work authorization if OPT is active when the I-129 is filed.
- If OPT expired before the I-129 filing but the student is still within the 60-day grace period, only F-1 status is extended (no work authorization).

### STEM OPT Extension (24 months)

- Students on the STEM OPT extension also qualify for cap-gap, following the same rules.
- The STEM OPT extension gives students up to **3 chances** to participate in the H-1B lottery (one during initial OPT and two during the 24-month STEM extension).
- Cap-gap can extend STEM OPT authorization beyond the printed EAD end date.

### If Not Selected in the H-1B Lottery

| Your OPT Status | Options |
|---|---|
| Initial 12-month OPT still active | Continue working; apply for STEM OPT extension if eligible |
| STEM OPT still active | Continue working; try again next year |
| OPT/STEM OPT expiring soon | Explore cap-exempt employers, change of status to another visa, or departure |
| OPT already expired | 60-day grace period to depart or change status |

### Unemployment Day Counting During Cap-Gap

Unemployment days continue to accrue during the cap-gap period if the student is not working. The standard limits still apply:

- **90 days** for post-completion OPT
- **150 days total** for OPT + STEM OPT combined

Exceeding the unemployment limit during cap-gap can result in termination of F-1 status.

## 7. Work Authorization During Cap-Gap

### When You CAN Work

- Your post-completion OPT or STEM OPT was **active and valid** on the date your employer filed the I-129.
- The H-1B petition has been **receipted** by USCIS (you have an I-797C receipt notice).
- The petition has not been denied, withdrawn, revoked, or rejected.
- You are working for the **petitioning employer** (the one who filed your H-1B).

### When You CANNOT Work

- Your OPT had already expired when the I-129 was filed (even if you were in the 60-day grace period -- status is extended but work authorization is not).
- The H-1B petition has been denied, withdrawn, or revoked.
- You are in the 60-day grace period after cap-gap termination (no work permitted during grace period).
- You want to work for a **different employer** than the H-1B petitioner (cap-gap work authorization is employer-specific for the petitioning employer).

### Important: Status Extension vs. Work Authorization Extension

These are two separate things:

| Scenario | F-1 Status | Work Authorization |
|---|---|---|
| OPT active at time of I-129 filing | Extended | Extended |
| OPT expired, filed during 60-day grace period | Extended | **NOT extended** |
| OPT active, but H-1B later denied | Terminates (60-day grace) | Terminates immediately |

## 8. Travel During the Cap-Gap Period

**The single most important rule: DO NOT travel internationally while a Change of Status petition is pending.**

### Why Travel Is Dangerous

- If you leave the U.S. while a COS petition is pending, USCIS considers the COS request **abandoned**.
- Your cap-gap extension is **voided**.
- You cannot re-enter the U.S. on F-1 status under cap-gap provisions.
- You would need to apply for an H-1B visa stamp at a U.S. consulate abroad (consular processing) -- which requires an approved H-1B petition and carries its own risks of denial.

### Travel Scenario Outcomes

| Scenario | Risk Level | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Travel before H-1B petition is filed | Moderate | OPT travel rules apply (need valid EAD, I-20 with travel signature, valid visa stamp) |
| Travel after I-129 filed but before approval | **Extremely high** | COS is deemed abandoned; cap-gap voided; cannot re-enter on F-1 cap-gap |
| Travel after H-1B approved (COS granted), before Oct 1 | High | Need valid H-1B visa stamp to re-enter; F-1 status has ended |
| Travel after Oct 1 with approved H-1B | Standard H-1B rules | Need valid H-1B visa stamp |

### Additional Risk: Presidential Proclamation Fee

As of FY 2026, foreign nationals seeking a change of status to H-1B must remain in the United States from filing to approval to avoid potentially triggering additional fees (including the $100,000 H-1B fee under recent presidential proclamations). Departure may be interpreted as consular processing, which carries different fee structures.

## 9. Key Dates and Deadlines

### Annual H-1B Cap Cycle (Typical Timeline)

| When | What | Action Required |
|---|---|---|
| **February - March** | H-1B electronic registration period | Employer registers; student provides documents |
| **Late March** | Lottery selection notifications | Check myUSCIS account |
| **April 1 - June 30** | H-1B filing window (for selected registrations) | Employer files I-129 with COS request |
| **Filing date** | Cap-gap begins (if eligible) | Verify SEVIS record is updated |
| **June 30** | Common OPT end date (May graduates) | Ensure I-129 is filed before OPT + grace period expires |
| **October 1** | H-1B start date | If approved: transition to H-1B. If pending: cap-gap continues |
| **April 1 (following year)** | Cap-gap expiration (under 2025 rule) | If still pending, cap-gap ends; consult attorney immediately |

### Critical Filing Deadlines

- The I-129 must be **received by USCIS** before the student's authorized F-1 period expires (including OPT and the 60-day grace period).
- For work authorization extension, the I-129 must be filed while OPT is still **active** (not during the grace period).
- H-1B registration typically occurs in **early March** each year -- check USCIS announcements for exact dates.

## 10. Common Misunderstandings

### Misunderstanding 1: "Lottery selection = cap-gap protection"

**Wrong.** Being selected in the H-1B lottery does **not** trigger the cap-gap extension. The employer must actually **file the I-129 petition** and USCIS must **receipt it**. Until that happens, there is no cap-gap.

### Misunderstanding 2: "Cap-gap applies to consular processing petitions"

**Wrong.** Cap-gap only applies when the H-1B petition requests a **change of status** (COS). If the employer files for consular processing (meaning the student would leave the U.S. and get an H-1B visa stamp abroad), there is **no cap-gap extension**. This is a common error -- some employers file consular processing by mistake, which voids cap-gap eligibility.

### Misunderstanding 3: "I can work during the 60-day grace period if I have cap-gap"

**It depends.** If your OPT was still active when the I-129 was filed, yes -- your work authorization is extended. But if your OPT had already expired and the petition was filed during your 60-day grace period, your F-1 status is extended but your work authorization is **not**. You may legally remain in the U.S. but you may not work.

### Misunderstanding 4: "I can travel and come back during cap-gap"

**Extremely risky.** Departing the U.S. while a COS petition is pending causes USCIS to deem the COS request abandoned. Your cap-gap extension ends, and you cannot re-enter on F-1 status under cap-gap. This is one of the most consequential mistakes a student can make.

### Misunderstanding 5: "Cap-gap means I can work for any employer"

**Wrong.** Cap-gap work authorization is tied to the **petitioning employer** -- the one who filed your H-1B. You cannot use cap-gap to work for a different employer unless they also file a separate cap-subject H-1B petition on your behalf.

### Misunderstanding 6: "My SEVIS record will update automatically -- I don't need to do anything"

**Partially true.** SEVIS should auto-update when USCIS data is transmitted, but this does not always happen. Students should proactively confirm with their DSO that the cap-gap extension appears in SEVIS. If it does not, the DSO can request a data fix through the SEVP Response Center.

### Misunderstanding 7: "Once my H-1B is approved, I automatically switch to H-1B status"

**Not exactly.** If the H-1B is approved with COS, your F-1 status ends and H-1B status begins on the petition start date (usually October 1). Your SEVIS record will be completed, and your DSO can no longer make changes to it. This transition is **irreversible** -- you cannot revert back to F-1 status.

### Misunderstanding 8: "The cap-gap extension always runs through April 1"

**Not always.** The cap-gap ends on the **earliest** of: (a) April 1 of the fiscal year, (b) the H-1B petition approval date / H-1B start date, or (c) the date the petition is denied/withdrawn/revoked. It is a maximum end date, not a guaranteed duration.

---

## Summary: Cap-Gap Checklist

- [ ] Confirm your employer is filing H-1B with **change of status** (not consular processing)
- [ ] Ensure I-129 is filed and receipted **before** your OPT + grace period expires
- [ ] Verify your SEVIS record shows the cap-gap extension (check with DSO)
- [ ] **Do not travel** internationally while the COS petition is pending
- [ ] Keep a copy of your I-797C receipt notice as proof of pending petition
- [ ] Monitor your H-1B case status regularly on the USCIS website
- [ ] If your petition is denied/withdrawn, you have 60 days to depart (no work during this period)
- [ ] Continue meeting OPT employment requirements (unemployment day limits still apply)
