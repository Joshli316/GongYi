# STEM OPT 24-Month Extension: Comprehensive Rules Guide

**Date:** April 7, 2026
**Sources:** USCIS, ICE SEVP, DHS Study in the States, Federal Register

---

## 1. Overview

The 24-month STEM OPT extension allows F-1 students who earned qualifying STEM degrees to extend their post-completion Optional Practical Training (OPT) by an additional 24 months beyond the standard 12-month OPT period. This means eligible students can work in the U.S. for up to 36 total months of OPT.

The extension was established through a 2016 DHS final rule (81 FR 13040) and is codified at 8 CFR 214.16. It replaced the previous 17-month STEM extension.

Key facts:

- **Total work authorization:** Up to 36 months (12 initial + 24 extension)
- **Lifetime limit:** Maximum of two STEM OPT extensions per student
- **Start date:** Begins the day after initial post-completion OPT expires, regardless of approval date
- **Duration:** Fixed, uninterrupted 24 consecutive months

## 2. Eligibility Requirements

### Degree Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|---|---|
| **Degree level** | Bachelor's, Master's, or Doctoral degree |
| **School accreditation** | Must be from a school accredited by a U.S. Department of Education-recognized accrediting agency |
| **SEVP certification** | School must be SEVP-certified at the time the student submits the STEM OPT application |
| **CIP code** | Degree program must appear on the DHS STEM Designated Degree Program List at the time of application |
| **Recency** | Qualifying STEM degree must have been received within 10 years of applying |

### CIP Code Requirements

The DHS STEM Designated Degree Program List uses the Department of Education's Classification of Instructional Programs (CIP) taxonomy. Four primary 2-digit CIP series are designated at the broadest level:

- **14** -- Engineering
- **26** -- Biological and Biomedical Sciences
- **27** -- Mathematics and Statistics
- **40** -- Physical Sciences

Additional fields are designated at the 6-digit CIP code level across 18+ related CIP series (e.g., Computer Science 11.0701, Data Science 30.7001). The complete list includes hundreds of eligible programs.

### Using a Prior STEM Degree

A student does not need to be on OPT based on a STEM degree -- they can use a **prior** qualifying STEM degree if:

- Their most recent degree (which triggered the current OPT) was earned from a currently accredited, SEVP-certified school
- The prior STEM degree was earned at the bachelor's level or higher
- The prior STEM degree was received within 10 years of the STEM OPT application
- The prior degree's CIP code appears on the current STEM Designated Degree Program List

### Student Status Requirements

- Must currently be in a valid period of post-completion OPT
- Must be in valid F-1 status
- Must not have exceeded the unemployment day limit

### Employer Requirements (for eligibility)

- Employer must be enrolled in **E-Verify**
- Employment must be at least **20 hours per week**
- A completed **Form I-983** training plan must be in place

## 3. Application Process and Required Forms

### Required Documents

| Form/Document | Purpose |
|---|---|
| **Form I-765** | Application for Employment Authorization (filed with USCIS) |
| **Form I-983** | Training Plan for STEM OPT Students (completed jointly by student and employer) |
| **Form I-20** | Certificate of Eligibility, endorsed by DSO with STEM OPT recommendation within the last 60 days |
| **STEM degree evidence** | Transcript or diploma showing qualifying STEM degree |
| **Passport photos** | Two passport-style photos |
| **Previous EAD** | Copy of current EAD card |
| **Employer's E-Verify number** | E-Verify Company ID or valid Client Company ID |

### Filing Category

STEM OPT extension uses eligibility category **C03C** on Form I-765.

### Application Steps

1. Student secures qualifying STEM OPT employment with an E-Verify employer
2. Student and employer complete Form I-983 together
3. Student submits Form I-983 to their DSO
4. DSO reviews Form I-983 for completeness and recommends STEM OPT in SEVIS
5. DSO issues updated Form I-20 with STEM OPT recommendation
6. Student files Form I-765 (c)(3)(C) with USCIS along with supporting documents
7. USCIS adjudicates the application

### Premium Processing

As of April 3, 2023, Form I-765 filings by F-1 students for STEM OPT (category C03C) are eligible for premium processing via Form I-907.

## 4. Filing Timeline

### Critical Deadlines

| Deadline | Rule |
|---|---|
| **Earliest filing** | Up to 90 days before current OPT expiration |
| **Latest filing** | Before OPT expiration date |
| **DSO recommendation window** | I-765 must be filed within 60 days of the DSO entering the STEM OPT recommendation in SEVIS |
| **I-20 freshness** | Must be endorsed by DSO within the last 60 days |

Both conditions must be satisfied simultaneously: (1) within 90 days before OPT expiration AND (2) within 60 days of the DSO's SEVIS recommendation.

### Practical Timeline Example

If OPT expires on August 15:
- Earliest filing: May 17 (90 days before)
- DSO should enter recommendation no earlier than ~June 16 (so the 60-day window covers the filing date)
- Student must file before August 15

## 5. Automatic 180-Day Extension While Pending

If a student files their STEM OPT extension application **on time** (before OPT expiration), they receive an **automatic 180-day extension** of their employment authorization while the application is pending.

Key rules:

- The 180-day auto-extension begins on the day after the initial OPT expires
- The student may continue working for the same employer during this period
- The extension ceases once USCIS adjudicates the application (approval or denial)
- If USCIS has not decided within 180 days, employment authorization ends until a decision is made
- The student remains in valid F-1 status during this period
- The employer can use the expired EAD card combined with the Form I-797C receipt notice as evidence of work authorization for I-9 purposes

## 6. Employer Requirements

### E-Verify Enrollment

- The employer **must** be enrolled in E-Verify before the student begins working
- The employer must remain in good standing with E-Verify throughout the STEM OPT period
- The employer must provide their E-Verify Company Identification Number or valid E-Verify Client Company Identification Number

### Bona Fide Employer-Employee Relationship

The employer must maintain a genuine employer-employee relationship, meaning:

- The employer provides and controls the means and manner of work
- The employer is not the student's "employer" in name only
- The employer has sufficient resources and trained personnel to provide appropriate training
- The student is not working on a "volunteer" basis

### Training and Supervision Obligations

On the Form I-983, the employer must attest that:

- They have enough resources and trained personnel to appropriately train the student
- The student will **not replace** a full- or part-time, temporary or permanent U.S. worker
- Working for them will help the student attain training objectives
- They will provide a structured, work-based learning experience

### Site Visit Compliance

DHS/ICE may conduct site visits of employers that employ STEM OPT students to verify:

- The employer possesses and maintains the ability and resources to provide training
- The student's actual work is consistent with the Form I-983 training plan
- Employers receive **advance notification** before a site visit
- Site visits are conducted by ICE federal employees
- DHS may also request information via email or phone instead of a physical visit
- DHS may refer matters to the Department of Labor or other agencies if warranted

## 7. Commensurate Compensation Requirement

STEM OPT students **must receive compensation** for their work. The regulation requires that compensation be **commensurate** -- meaning comparable to what similarly situated U.S. workers receive.

### What "Commensurate" Means

- Duties, hours, and compensation must be commensurate with those of the employer's similarly situated U.S. workers in the area of employment
- "Similarly situated" means workers with similar duties, qualifications, experience, and education level
- The employer must document compensation details on Form I-983, including dollar amount, payment frequency, and justification

### Key Restrictions

- **No unpaid work:** Volunteer positions do not satisfy STEM OPT extension requirements
- **No below-market pay:** Compensation cannot be significantly below what comparable U.S. workers earn
- **Pay reductions must be reported:** Any reduction in compensation not tied to a reduction in hours constitutes a material change requiring a modified Form I-983

## 8. Multiple STEM Extensions

### Lifetime Limit

A student may receive **a maximum of two** STEM OPT extensions in their lifetime.

### How to Qualify for a Second Extension

To obtain a second STEM OPT extension, a student must:

1. **Earn a new qualifying degree** at a higher education level than the degree used for the first STEM OPT extension (e.g., first extension based on a bachelor's, second based on a master's)
2. **Be in a valid period of initial post-completion OPT** at the time they apply for the second STEM OPT extension
3. Meet all other standard eligibility requirements

### Prior Degree Option for Second Extension

A student may use a prior STEM degree for the second extension as well, provided:
- The prior degree is at a higher level than what was used for the first extension
- The degree was received within 10 years
- The CIP code is on the current STEM Designated Degree Program List

### Example Scenario

| Extension | Degree Used | OPT Based On |
|---|---|---|
| 1st STEM OPT | BS in Computer Science | BS in Computer Science |
| 2nd STEM OPT | MS in Data Science (or prior BS if earning a non-STEM MS) | MS degree program |

## 9. Employer Change During STEM OPT

STEM OPT students may change employers, but must follow specific procedures.

### Steps for Changing Employers

1. The **new employer must be enrolled in E-Verify** before the student begins working
2. Student and new employer complete a **new Form I-983**
3. Student submits the new Form I-983 to their DSO **within 10 days** of starting the new position
4. DSO reviews for completeness and updates SEVIS with a new recommendation
5. DSO reports the change of employer in SEVIS

### Important Rules

- Students do **not** need to file a new Form I-765 with USCIS when changing employers
- Students may work for **multiple concurrent employers** as long as each is E-Verify enrolled and each has its own Form I-983
- Unemployment days between jobs count toward the 150-day limit
- Each new employer must meet all STEM OPT employer requirements

## 10. Self-Employment Restrictions

### Strict Prohibition

**Self-employment is not permitted** on STEM OPT. Unlike standard post-completion OPT (which allows self-employment), the STEM OPT extension prohibits it.

Key restrictions:

- Students **cannot** be their own employer
- Students **cannot** fill out their own Form I-983 (Sections 3-6)
- Students **cannot** sign as both student and employer on the training plan
- A bona fide employer-employee relationship requires a separate employer entity

### Entrepreneurial Exception (Limited)

Students **may** participate in a start-up or entrepreneurial venture, but only if:

- There is a **separate, legitimate employer** (not the student themselves)
- The employer has sufficient resources and personnel to train the student
- The student is an **employee** of the company, not the sole proprietor
- Someone other than the student completes and signs the employer sections of Form I-983
- The arrangement satisfies the bona fide employer-employee relationship test

## 11. Reporting Requirements

### Student Reporting Obligations

| Requirement | Frequency/Deadline |
|---|---|
| **Validation reporting** | Every 6 months (at 6, 12, and 18 months) |
| **Name or address change** | Report to DSO within 10 days |
| **New employer** | Submit new I-983 to DSO within 10 days of start |
| **Loss of employment** | Report to DSO immediately |
| **Material changes to training plan** | Report to DSO at earliest opportunity |

### Six-Month Validation Reports

Every 6 months, the student must confirm with their DSO that SEVIS accurately reflects:
- Legal name
- Residential or mailing address
- Employer name and address
- Status of current employment

### Employer Reporting Obligations

- Report material changes to the training plan by submitting a modified Form I-983
- Report **termination of the student's employment** or departure to the DSO **within 5 business days**
- Material changes include: change of employer EIN, reduction in compensation not tied to hours, significant changes in duties or training objectives

### Unemployment Day Limits

| Period | Allowed Unemployment Days |
|---|---|
| Initial 12-month OPT | 90 days |
| STEM OPT extension (additional) | 60 days |
| **Total (combined)** | **150 days** |

Exceeding the unemployment limit results in loss of F-1 status.

## 12. Recent Policy Changes (2024-2026)

### STEM Designated Degree Program List Update (July 2024)

DHS published a Federal Register notice (89 FR 59318, July 22, 2024) adding **Environmental/Natural Resource Economics (CIP code 03.0204)** to the STEM Designated Degree Program List.

### USCIS Policy Manual Update (August 2024)

USCIS updated guidance to:
- Clarify that students must file the I-765 within 60 days of the DSO's SEVIS recommendation (aligning policy manual with regulations)
- Clarify guidance on online study, school transfers, the grace period, and study abroad as they relate to OPT eligibility

### H-1B Cap-Gap Extension to April 1 (January 2025)

Effective January 17, 2025, the DHS final H-1B rule extended the cap-gap provision:

| Before | After |
|---|---|
| F-1 status and OPT/STEM OPT auto-extended to October 1 | Extended to **April 1** of the relevant fiscal year |

This change applies to F-1 students who are beneficiaries of a timely filed, cap-subject H-1B petition requesting change of status. It prevents gaps in status and work authorization caused by USCIS processing delays. It first applied to the FY 2026 H-1B registration period.

Students on STEM OPT who are in a cap-gap extension period may apply for a STEM OPT extension during that period, but **not** after the cap-gap ends and the 60-day grace period begins.

### Premium Processing Availability

Since April 2023, all Form I-765 filings in category C03C (STEM OPT) are eligible for premium processing, significantly reducing wait times for adjudication.
