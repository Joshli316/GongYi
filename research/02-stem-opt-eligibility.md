# STEM OPT Eligibility Requirements: Comprehensive Guide

*Research Date: April 7, 2026*

---

## 1. What Is the STEM OPT Extension?

The STEM OPT extension allows eligible F-1 students who have earned a degree in a science, technology, engineering, or mathematics (STEM) field to apply for a **24-month extension** of their post-completion Optional Practical Training (OPT). This extends the standard 12-month OPT period to a total of up to **36 months** of work authorization in the United States.

The extension was established under the 2016 Final Rule (81 FR 13039) and is codified at 8 CFR 214.16.

**Key facts:**

- Available only to F-1 visa holders
- Requires a qualifying STEM degree at the bachelor's, master's, or doctoral level
- Limited to **two STEM OPT extensions per lifetime**, each based on a different qualifying degree
- The qualifying STEM degree must have been earned within **10 years** of the STEM OPT extension application

## 2. DHS STEM Designated Degree Program List

### What It Is

The DHS STEM Designated Degree Program List is the official, exhaustive list of degree fields that qualify a student for the 24-month STEM OPT extension. A degree must appear on this list **at the time the student submits their STEM OPT application** (not at the time the degree was earned).

### Where to Find It

| Resource | URL |
|----------|-----|
| Current PDF list | [ice.gov/doclib/sevis/pdf/stemList2024.pdf](https://www.ice.gov/doclib/sevis/pdf/stemList2024.pdf) |
| Eligible CIP codes page | [studyinthestates.dhs.gov/stem-opt-hub/additional-resources/eligible-cip-codes-for-the-stem-opt-extension](https://studyinthestates.dhs.gov/stem-opt-hub/additional-resources/eligible-cip-codes-for-the-stem-opt-extension) |
| Federal Register updates | [federalregister.gov](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2024/07/23/2024-16127/update-to-the-department-of-homeland-security-stem-designated-degree-program-list) |

### Structure

The list is organized into two tiers:

- **Four primary CIP series** (all 6-digit codes within these 2-digit categories qualify):
  - 14 -- Engineering
  - 26 -- Biological and Biomedical Sciences
  - 27 -- Mathematics and Statistics
  - 40 -- Physical Sciences

- **18 related CIP series** (only specific 6-digit codes qualify):
  - 01 -- Agriculture/Animal/Plant/Veterinary Science
  - 03 -- Natural Resources and Conservation
  - 04 -- Architecture and Related Services
  - 09 -- Communication and Journalism
  - 10 -- Communications Technologies
  - 11 -- Computer and Information Sciences
  - 13 -- Education
  - 15 -- Engineering Technologies
  - 28 -- Military Science, Leadership
  - 29 -- Military Technologies
  - 30 -- Multi/Interdisciplinary Studies
  - 41 -- Science Technologies/Technicians
  - 42 -- Psychology
  - 43 -- Homeland Security/Law Enforcement
  - 45 -- Social Sciences
  - 49 -- Transportation and Materials Moving
  - 51 -- Health Professions
  - 52 -- Business, Management, Marketing

### How Often Updated

SEVP reviews nominations on an **annual cycle**. The nomination deadline is typically **August 1** each year. DHS publishes updates via Federal Register notices. No CIP codes have been removed from the list since its creation; codes are only added.

## 3. CIP Codes: What They Are and How to Check

### What Is a CIP Code?

A Classification of Instructional Programs (CIP) code is a 6-digit taxonomic code (format: XX.XXXX) assigned by the National Center for Education Statistics (NCES) to classify academic programs. Every degree program at a SEVP-certified school is assigned a CIP code in the Student and Exchange Visitor Information System (SEVIS).

### How Students Find Their CIP Code

1. **Check your I-20 form** -- The CIP code for your degree program is listed on your Form I-20 (Certificate of Eligibility for Nonimmigrant Student Status)
2. **Ask your DSO** -- Your Designated School Official can look up your CIP code in SEVIS
3. **Check SEVIS records** -- Your school's SEVIS record contains the CIP code linked to your program

### How to Verify Eligibility

1. Find your 6-digit CIP code from your I-20 or DSO
2. Check it against the [DHS STEM Designated Degree Program List](https://www.ice.gov/doclib/sevis/pdf/stemList2024.pdf)
3. If your CIP code falls within the four primary series (14, 26, 27, 40), all 6-digit codes qualify
4. If your CIP code is in one of the 18 related series, you must confirm the specific 6-digit code appears on the list
5. The CIP code must be on the list **at the time you apply**, not when you graduated

## 4. E-Verify Requirement for Employers

### The Rule

The employer providing the STEM OPT training opportunity **must be enrolled in E-Verify** at the time the student begins work. E-Verify is a web-based system operated by USCIS that allows employers to confirm the employment eligibility of their employees.

### Key Details

- The E-Verify requirement applies to the **specific employer entity** (identified by EIN) employing the student
- If a student changes employers during STEM OPT, the **new employer must also be enrolled in E-Verify**
- Students can verify an employer's E-Verify participation through the [E-Verify employer search tool](https://www.e-verify.gov/)
- Staffing agencies that place students at third-party worksites must themselves be enrolled in E-Verify; the client company's enrollment alone is not sufficient

### Why It Matters

Without E-Verify enrollment, the employer cannot sign the Form I-983, and the student cannot qualify for STEM OPT through that employer. This is a hard requirement with no exceptions.

## 5. Educational Requirements

### Degree Requirements

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Degree level** | Bachelor's, master's, or doctoral degree |
| **School accreditation** | Must be accredited by a U.S. Department of Education-recognized accrediting agency |
| **SEVP certification** | The school must be SEVP-certified at the time the student submits the STEM OPT application |
| **STEM designation** | The degree's CIP code must appear on the DHS STEM Designated Degree Program List |
| **Recency** | The qualifying STEM degree must have been earned within 10 years of the application date |

### Prior Degree Option

Students may use a **prior STEM degree** to qualify for STEM OPT, even if their most recent degree is not STEM. Requirements:

- The most recent degree must be from an accredited, SEVP-certified school
- The prior STEM degree must be at the bachelor's level or higher
- The prior STEM degree must have been earned within 10 years
- The prior STEM degree's CIP code must be on the current DHS STEM Designated Degree Program List
- The student must be on a period of post-completion OPT related to their most recent degree

### Two Lifetime Limit

A student may receive the STEM OPT extension **a maximum of two times** over the course of their academic career. Each extension must be based on a **different qualifying degree** (e.g., one based on a bachelor's and one based on a master's).

## 6. Employment Requirements

### Direct Relationship to Degree

The practical training opportunity must be **directly related** to the student's qualifying STEM degree. This is not a loose connection -- the Form I-983 must specifically articulate:

- How the job duties relate to the STEM degree
- Specific knowledge, skills, or techniques from the degree that will be applied
- How the training advances the student's learning objectives in the STEM field

### Work Hours

- Minimum of **20 hours per week** (part-time is allowed, but not fewer than 20 hours)
- Full-time employment is not required

### Paid Employment

- The position must be a **paid position** -- volunteer work does not qualify
- Compensation must be commensurate with similarly situated U.S. workers (to prevent undercutting)

### Unemployment Limits

- Students on STEM OPT receive a total of **150 days of allowable unemployment** (90 days during initial OPT + 60 additional days during the STEM extension)
- Unemployment during cap-gap extensions counts toward this limit
- Exceeding the unemployment limit is a violation of F-1 status

## 7. Employer Requirements

### Bona Fide Employer

The employer must be a **bona fide employer**, meaning:

- The employer cannot be the student's "employer" in name only
- The student cannot work on a volunteer basis
- The employer that signs the Form I-983 must be the **same entity** providing the practical training

### Supervision and Training

The employer must have:

- **Sufficient resources** and **trained supervisory personnel** to provide appropriate training
- Supervision at the location(s) where the practical training takes place
- Supervisory personnel who are employees of the employer or contractors retained directly by the employer -- **not employees of the employer's clients or customers**

### Form I-983 Training Plan

The employer and student must jointly complete the [Form I-983](https://www.ice.gov/doclib/sevis/pdf/i983.pdf), which must include:

- Specific learning objectives and goals
- Explanation of how goals will be achieved
- Performance evaluation process
- Methods of oversight and supervision
- How the training directly relates to the student's qualifying STEM degree

### Reporting Obligations

Employers must:

- Report material changes to the training plan within 10 days
- Co-sign the student's annual self-evaluation
- Submit a new Form I-983 for any material change (e.g., change in EIN, compensation reduction, significant decrease in hours, changes to learning objectives)
- Cooperate with DHS site visits, which may occur at DHS's discretion

## 8. Filing Timeline

### Application Window

| Step | Timeline |
|------|----------|
| Complete Form I-983 with employer | Before requesting DSO recommendation |
| DSO enters STEM OPT recommendation in SEVIS | Before filing I-765 |
| File Form I-765 with USCIS | Up to **90 days before** current OPT expiration, and within **60 days** of DSO's SEVIS recommendation |
| USCIS must receive I-765 | **Before** the current OPT end date |

### Critical Deadlines

- You **cannot apply** during the 60-day grace period after OPT expires -- the application must be received by USCIS before your OPT end date
- If you miss the deadline, you lose eligibility for that STEM OPT extension

### Processing Times

- Standard processing: Variable; check [USCIS Processing Times](https://egov.uscis.gov/processing-times/) for current estimates
- **Premium processing**: Available for Form I-765 STEM OPT applications. USCIS adjudicates within **30 business days**. Fees increased effective March 1, 2026 -- check the [USCIS Fee Schedule](https://www.uscis.gov/g-1055) for current amounts
- **Automatic 180-day extension**: If you file on time and your OPT expires while the STEM OPT application is pending, your employment authorization is automatically extended for up to **180 days** while USCIS adjudicates

### Required Documents for Filing

- Form I-765 (Application for Employment Authorization)
- Form I-983 (Training Plan, signed by student and employer)
- Copy of STEM degree and transcripts
- Copy of current EAD card
- Passport-style photos
- Filing fee (check current amount at uscis.gov)
- Evidence of employer's E-Verify enrollment

## 9. Who Is NOT Eligible

### Ineligibility Factors

- **Non-STEM degrees**: Students whose degree CIP code is not on the DHS STEM Designated Degree Program List
- **Associate's degrees or certificates**: Only bachelor's, master's, and doctoral degrees qualify
- **Unaccredited schools**: Degrees from schools not accredited by a USDOE-recognized accrediting agency
- **Non-SEVP-certified schools**: The school must be SEVP-certified at the time of application
- **Degree older than 10 years**: The qualifying STEM degree must have been earned within the 10-year window
- **Already used two STEM OPT extensions**: Students who have already received two STEM OPT extensions on different degrees
- **Applying during 60-day grace period**: Students who let their OPT expire before filing
- **Exceeded unemployment limits**: Students who have exceeded 90 days of unemployment during initial OPT (or 150 days total if previously on STEM OPT)
- **Status violations**: Students found to have violated their F-1 status, committed fraud, or made misrepresentations
- **Employer not E-Verify enrolled**: Even if the student qualifies, the application will be denied if the employer is not in E-Verify
- **Self-employed students**: STEM OPT does not permit self-employment; the student must work for an E-Verify employer

### Important: No Country-Based Restrictions

STEM OPT eligibility is **not based on nationality or country of origin**. Students from all countries are eligible, provided they meet all other requirements. There is no country quota or restriction for STEM OPT (unlike the H-1B lottery, which is nationality-neutral but perception-affected by demand patterns).

## 10. Recent Changes and Updates

### 2024 Updates

- **July 22, 2024**: DHS published a Federal Register notice adding one new CIP code: **Environmental/Natural Resource Economics (03.0204)**. This was the result of reviewing 133 public nominations. No CIP codes were removed. ([Federal Register Notice](https://www.federalregister.gov/documents/2024/07/23/2024-16127/update-to-the-department-of-homeland-security-stem-designated-degree-program-list))

### 2023 Updates

- DHS added **eight new fields of study** to the list, including:
  - Mechatronics, Robotics, and Automation Engineering Technology/Technician
  - Linguistics and Computer Science
  - Developmental and Adolescent Psychology
  - Several other interdisciplinary STEM fields

### 2026 Fee Changes

- **March 1, 2026**: USCIS premium processing fees increased to reflect inflation from June 2023 through June 2025. Students filing premium processing requests on or after this date must pay the updated fee. ([USCIS Fee Update](https://www.uscis.gov/newsroom/alerts/uscis-to-increase-premium-processing-fees))

### Nomination Cycle

- SEVP accepts public nominations for new CIP codes to be added to the STEM Designated Degree Program List on an annual cycle
- Nominations are typically due by **August 1** each year
- Any person or organization can submit a nomination
