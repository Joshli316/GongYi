# F-1 OPT Reporting Requirements to Your DSO

> A comprehensive guide to what, when, and how to report during Optional Practical Training.

**Last updated:** April 2026
**Regulatory basis:** 8 CFR 214.2(f)(12), 8 CFR 214.2(f)(10)(ii)(C)

---

## 1. Overview: Why Reporting Matters

During OPT (both standard post-completion and STEM extension), you remain in F-1 status. Your Designated School Official (DSO) is legally required to keep your SEVIS record current. Failure to report changes can result in SEVIS record termination, loss of work authorization, and jeopardized immigration status.

The 10-day rule is the backbone: **you must report most changes within 10 days of the event.**

---

## 2. What Must Be Reported During OPT

### Standard Post-Completion OPT

| Reportable Event | Deadline | How to Report |
|---|---|---|
| New employment | Within 10 days of start | SEVP Portal or DSO |
| Loss of employment | Within 10 days | SEVP Portal or DSO |
| Change of employer | Within 10 days | SEVP Portal or DSO |
| Change of address | Within 10 days | SEVP Portal or DSO |
| Change of phone number | Within 10 days | SEVP Portal |
| Change of legal name | Within 10 days | Contact DSO directly |
| Interruption of employment | Within 10 days | SEVP Portal or DSO |

### STEM OPT Extension (additional requirements)

| Reportable Event | Deadline | How to Report |
|---|---|---|
| Six-month validation report | Every 6 months from STEM OPT start | SEVP Portal or DSO |
| Annual self-evaluation (12 months) | Within 10 days after the 12-month mark | Submit to DSO |
| Final self-evaluation (24 months) | Within 10 days of STEM OPT end | Submit to DSO |
| Material change to training plan | At earliest available opportunity | New Form I-983 to DSO |
| Employer termination of employment | Employer: within 5 business days; Student: within 10 days | Employer notifies DSO; student reports to DSO |

## 3. Employment Changes in Detail

### Starting a New Job

You must report:
- Employer name
- Employer address (the location where you physically work, not corporate HQ if different)
- Start date
- Brief description of how the job relates to your major/field of study

For **standard OPT**: Report via the SEVP Portal within 10 days of your start date. You can add employer information yourself.

For **STEM OPT**: You **cannot** use the SEVP Portal's "Add Employer" feature. Instead, you must complete a Form I-983 (Training Plan) with your employer and submit it to your DSO, who enters the employer information into SEVIS.

### Losing a Job / Employment Ends

- Report within **10 days** of your last day of employment
- Update the end date for that employer in the SEVP Portal (standard OPT) or notify your DSO (STEM OPT)
- Do **not** delete the employer record -- just add an end date. Deleting records affects your unemployment counter.

### Changing Employers

This is effectively ending one job and starting another:
1. Report the end date of the previous employer within 10 days
2. Report the new employer information within 10 days of starting
3. For STEM OPT: submit a new Form I-983 for the new employer to your DSO

### Multiple Employers

You may work for multiple employers simultaneously. Each must be individually reported with the information listed above.

---

## 4. Address Changes

**Deadline: 10 days from the date you move.**

This is a federal immigration requirement (not just a school policy). You must report your new **physical/residential address** -- the place where you actually live.

**How to report:**
- Update directly in the **SEVP Portal**, or
- Email/contact your DSO

Your DSO is then required to update SEVIS within 21 days of receiving the information.

---

## 5. Name Changes

If you legally change your name (e.g., through marriage, court order), you must report it to your DSO within **10 days**.

**How to report:**
- Contact your DSO directly (name changes typically cannot be made through the SEVP Portal by students)
- Provide legal documentation of the name change

The DSO will update your SEVIS record. During six-month validation reporting for STEM OPT, you must also confirm that your legal name in SEVIS is still correct.

---

## 6. Employer Information Requirements

### Standard OPT -- What You Report

| Field | Details |
|---|---|
| Company name | Full legal name of employer |
| Company address | Physical address where you work |
| Start date | First day of employment |
| End date | Last day (when applicable) |
| Job relation to major | A few sentences explaining how the work relates to your field of study |

### STEM OPT -- Additional Requirements (Form I-983)

| Field | Details |
|---|---|
| Employer Identification Number (EIN) | Required; must be valid IRS-issued EIN |
| Supervisor name | Full name of direct supervisor |
| Supervisor phone and email | Contact information for supervisor |
| Hours per week | Must be at least 20 hours/week |
| Compensation | Salary/wage information |
| Training goals and objectives | Detailed learning plan |
| Performance evaluation methods | How progress will be measured |
| E-Verify Company ID | Employer must be enrolled in E-Verify |

## 7. STEM OPT-Specific Reporting Requirements

### 7a. Six-Month Validation Reports

STEM OPT students must confirm the validity of their SEVIS information **every six months**, starting from the STEM OPT extension start date and continuing until the extension ends or F-1 status ends (whichever comes first).

**What you must confirm:**
- Legal name is correct
- Residential/mailing address is current
- Employer name and address are accurate
- Current employment status is correct

**How it works:**
- SEVP Portal users receive an **email reminder 30 days before** the validation report is due
- You can complete validation through the SEVP Portal or by contacting your DSO
- If you do not complete the validation, your DSO may not be able to confirm your status, risking SEVIS termination

### 7b. Annual Self-Evaluation (Form I-983)

STEM OPT students must complete and submit self-evaluations that describe the progress of the training experience:

| Evaluation | When Due | What It Covers |
|---|---|---|
| **12-month evaluation** | Within 10 days after the 12-month anniversary of STEM OPT start | Progress on training goals from the I-983 |
| **Final evaluation** | Within 10 days after STEM OPT period ends | Recap of all training and knowledge acquired during the full 24-month period |

**Requirements:**
- Both the student **and** the employer/supervisor must sign the self-evaluation
- Submit to your DSO, who stores it in your student record
- The evaluation should reference the goals and objectives from your original Form I-983

### 7c. Material Changes to Training Plan

A new Form I-983 must be submitted to the DSO at the **earliest available opportunity** when there is a material change to or deviation from the training plan. Material changes include:

- Change of employer's EIN (e.g., corporate restructuring)
- Reduction in compensation not tied to reduced hours
- Significant decrease in hours per week
- Changes to employer's commitments or student's learning objectives
- Any change that deviates from what was documented on the original I-983

**Important:** As long as the modified I-983 meets regulatory requirements, employment authorization will not cease based on the training plan change alone.

### 7d. Employer Termination Reporting

When a STEM OPT student's employment is terminated before the end of the authorized extension:
- The **employer** must notify the DSO within **5 business days** of the termination or the student's departure
- The **student** must report the loss of employment within **10 days**

## 8. Unemployment Limits and Tracking

| OPT Type | Maximum Unemployment Days |
|---|---|
| Standard post-completion OPT | 90 days total |
| STEM OPT extension | 150 days total (90 from initial OPT + 60 additional) |

**How unemployment is counted:**
- Every day without reported employment in SEVIS counts as an unemployment day
- If you do not report your employment, each unreported day is counted as unemployment
- The clock starts from your EAD start date (or approval date, whichever is later)

---

## 9. What Happens If You Fail to Report

### Consequences of Non-Reporting

| Failure | Consequence |
|---|---|
| Not reporting employment within 90 days of EAD start | SEVIS record **automatically terminated** |
| Exceeding 90 days unemployment (standard OPT) | SEVIS record terminated within 15 days |
| Exceeding 150 days unemployment (STEM OPT) | SEVIS record terminated within 15 days |
| Not reporting address change | Violation of F-1 status; may affect future immigration benefits |
| Not completing six-month validation (STEM OPT) | DSO cannot confirm status; risk of SEVIS termination |
| Not submitting annual self-evaluation (STEM OPT) | Violation of STEM OPT conditions |
| Not reporting material changes to I-983 | Violation of STEM OPT conditions |

### What SEVIS Termination Means

- Your F-1 status ends
- Your work authorization ends immediately
- You may be subject to removal proceedings
- It can negatively affect future visa applications
- Your school's SEVP certification may also be impacted

## 10. How to Report: Methods and Channels

### SEVP Portal (Primary Method for Students)

The SEVP Portal ([sevp.ice.gov/opt](https://sevp.ice.gov/opt)) allows post-completion OPT students to directly update:

| What You Can Update | Standard OPT | STEM OPT |
|---|---|---|
| Home address | Yes | Yes |
| Phone number | Yes | Yes |
| Add new employer | Yes | **No** (must go through DSO with I-983) |
| Edit existing employer | Yes | Limited |
| Six-month validation | N/A | Yes |

**Access:** You need your SEVIS ID and the email address on file in SEVIS to create/access your account.

### Contact Your DSO Directly

For anything you cannot do in the SEVP Portal, or if you prefer, contact your school's international student office:

- **Email** your DSO (most common method)
- **In-person** appointment
- **School-specific online forms** (many universities have dedicated OPT reporting forms on their websites)

### What Only the DSO Can Do

- Update your name in SEVIS
- Enter STEM OPT employer information (from Form I-983)
- Process I-983 training plans and self-evaluations
- Report program changes
- Update SEVIS within 21 days of receiving student-reported changes

## 11. Practical Checklist: All Reportable Events with Deadlines

### Immediate Actions (Within 10 Days)

- [ ] **New job starts** -- Report employer name, address, start date, job relation to major
- [ ] **Job ends** -- Report end date (do NOT delete the employer record)
- [ ] **Employer changes** -- Report end of old job + start of new job
- [ ] **Address changes** -- Update in SEVP Portal or notify DSO
- [ ] **Phone number changes** -- Update in SEVP Portal
- [ ] **Legal name changes** -- Notify DSO directly with documentation
- [ ] **Loss of employment** -- Report immediately; unemployment clock is ticking

### STEM OPT: At Earliest Opportunity

- [ ] **Material change to training plan** -- Submit new Form I-983 to DSO
- [ ] **Employer termination** -- Employer notifies DSO within 5 business days; you notify within 10 days

### STEM OPT: Recurring Deadlines

- [ ] **Every 6 months** -- Complete validation report confirming name, address, employer, employment status
- [ ] **At 12 months** -- Submit signed annual self-evaluation to DSO (within 10 days of the 12-month mark)
- [ ] **At 24 months (or end of STEM OPT)** -- Submit signed final self-evaluation to DSO (within 10 days)

### Ongoing Awareness

- [ ] **Track unemployment days** -- Standard OPT: max 90 days; STEM OPT: max 150 days
- [ ] **Keep copies** of all Forms I-983, self-evaluations, and correspondence with DSO
- [ ] **Monitor SEVP Portal** for validation reminders (sent 30 days before due)
- [ ] **Never delete employer records** from SEVP Portal unless entered by mistake
- [ ] **Maintain at least 20 hours/week** of employment related to your field of study

---

## Summary: The Three Rules of OPT Reporting

1. **10 days.** Almost everything must be reported within 10 days of the change.
2. **SEVP Portal first, DSO for the rest.** Use the portal for address and employment updates (standard OPT). Contact your DSO for name changes, STEM OPT employers, and I-983 submissions.
3. **Unreported days = unemployment days.** Every day without employment recorded in SEVIS counts against your limit.
