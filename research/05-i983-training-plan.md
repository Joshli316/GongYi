# Form I-983: STEM OPT Training Plan -- Complete Guide

## 1. What Is Form I-983 and When Is It Required

Form I-983, "Training Plan for STEM OPT Students," is a formal document required by the Department of Homeland Security (DHS) for any F-1 student applying for the 24-month STEM OPT extension. It serves as a structured agreement between the student, their employer, and the school's Designated School Official (DSO).

**When it is required:**

- Before a DSO can recommend a STEM OPT extension in SEVIS
- When starting a new job during an active STEM OPT period
- When there are material changes to an existing training plan
- At 12-month and 24-month evaluation milestones

The form cannot be submitted by the student alone. The employer must co-complete and sign it. Without a properly completed I-983 on file, the DSO cannot recommend the STEM OPT extension, and USCIS will not approve it.

---

## 2. Section-by-Section Breakdown

The I-983 is a 5-page form with distinct sections. Below is what each section covers and who is responsible.

| Section | Title | Completed By | Key Contents |
|---------|-------|-------------|--------------|
| **1** | Student Information | Student | Name, email, SEVIS ID, school info, STEM degree details, CIP code, requested OPT period |
| **2** | Student Certification | Student | Student signs certifying they understand the plan, will report changes, and will notify DSO of non-compliance |
| **3** | Employer Information | Employer | Company name, address, website, EIN (Federal Tax ID), NAICS code, number of full-time employees, employer point of contact |
| **4** | Employer Official Certification (Part 1) | Employer | Signatory authority certifies employer info is accurate; includes printed name, title, signature, and date |
| **5** | Training Plan Details | Student + Employer | The substantive core: job title, duties, STEM degree connection, learning objectives, supervision plan, evaluation methods, compensation details |
| **6** | Employer Official Certification (Part 2) | Employer | Employer attests to providing training, supervision, commensurate wages, and not displacing U.S. workers |
| **Eval (Page 5)** | Evaluation of Student Progress | Student + Employer | Two evaluation boxes: 12-month self-assessment and 24-month final assessment, both requiring employer review and signature |

### Section 3: Critical Employer Data Points

- **EIN**: 9-digit IRS number (format: ##-#######). Required -- no EIN means no valid I-983.
- **NAICS Code**: Found at census.gov. Classifies the employer's industry.
- **Number of employees**: Full-time count at the work site.
- **Compensation**: Must list annual salary or hourly wage. Must be commensurate with similarly situated U.S. workers.

### Section 5: Training Plan Core (Most Important Section)

This is where most I-983s succeed or fail. It must explain:

1. How the practical training **directly relates** to the student's qualifying STEM degree
2. **Specific goals** of the training opportunity
3. **How goals will be achieved** through work-based learning, including knowledge, skills, and techniques to be acquired
4. The **performance evaluation process**
5. **Methods of oversight and supervision**

## 3. Student vs. Employer Responsibilities

| Responsibility | Student | Employer |
|---------------|---------|----------|
| Complete Section 1 (personal info) | Yes | -- |
| Sign Section 2 (student certification) | Yes | -- |
| Complete Section 3 (company info) | -- | Yes |
| Sign Section 4 (employer certification) | -- | Yes |
| Draft Section 5 (training plan) | Collaborate | Collaborate |
| Sign Section 6 (employer attestations) | -- | Yes |
| Submit completed form to DSO | Yes | -- |
| Complete 12-month self-evaluation | Yes (write) | Yes (review + sign) |
| Complete 24-month final evaluation | Yes (write) | Yes (review + sign) |
| Report material changes | Yes (to DSO) | Yes (to DSO via student) |
| Maintain commensurate wages | -- | Yes |
| Provide supervision and mentoring | -- | Yes |
| Report termination of employment | -- | Yes (within 5 business days) |

**Key point**: The student is ultimately responsible for ensuring the form reaches the DSO. The employer is responsible for the substantive attestations about training quality, wages, and supervision.

## 4. Learning Objectives: How to Write Them

Learning objectives are the heart of the I-983. They appear in Section 5 and are the primary area DSOs scrutinize.

### What Makes Good Learning Objectives

- **Focus on student growth, not job duties.** Do not list what you do for the company. Describe what knowledge and skills you will gain.
- **Connect to your STEM degree.** Every objective should clearly tie back to your CIP code and degree field.
- **Be specific and measurable.** Avoid vague statements like "learn about software." Instead: "Develop proficiency in distributed systems architecture by designing and deploying microservices using Kubernetes."
- **Break into phases.** Structure objectives across the 24-month timeline (e.g., months 1-6, 7-12, 13-18, 19-24).

### Structure for Writing Objectives

Use this framework for each objective:

> **Skill/Knowledge Area** + **Method of Acquisition** + **Connection to STEM Degree**

### Example Objectives (Software Engineering, CS degree)

| Phase | Objective |
|-------|-----------|
| Months 1-6 | Develop proficiency in cloud infrastructure by assisting senior engineers in designing CI/CD pipelines, applying knowledge from coursework in distributed systems |
| Months 7-12 | Lead independent feature development using machine learning frameworks (TensorFlow, PyTorch), building on graduate research in statistical modeling |
| Months 13-18 | Participate in system architecture reviews and contribute to performance optimization, applying algorithms and data structures knowledge |
| Months 19-24 | Mentor junior team members and present technical findings to cross-functional teams, synthesizing full-stack engineering skills acquired during training |

### Action Verbs to Use

Design, develop, implement, analyze, evaluate, research, coordinate, participate in, observe, apply, build, optimize, architect, test, validate, integrate, document.

### What to Avoid

- Listing job duties ("answer emails," "attend meetings")
- Generic statements ("learn new things")
- Objectives unrelated to the STEM degree
- Copy-pasting the job description

## 5. Supervision and Mentoring Requirements

The I-983 requires employers to describe how they will provide **oversight and supervision** of the STEM OPT student. This is not optional -- it is a regulatory requirement.

### What the Form Requires

- Name and contact information of the direct supervisor
- Description of how the employer will provide oversight and supervision
- If the employer has a formal training program or policy, describing it may fulfill this requirement

### What to Include

- **Day-to-day supervision structure**: Who the student reports to, frequency of check-ins (e.g., weekly 1:1 meetings)
- **Mentoring relationship**: Identify a mentor who is experienced and knowledgeable in the STEM field
- **Project management methodology**: Agile sprints, code reviews, design reviews, etc.
- **Regular interactions**: Team standups, cross-functional collaboration, mentorship sessions
- **Company-wide policies**: Reference existing onboarding programs, performance review cycles, or professional development programs

### Regulatory Standard

The employer must have "enough resources and trained personnel available to appropriately train the student." The training must be provided by "experienced and knowledgeable staff."

## 6. Evaluation Requirements

The I-983 includes two mandatory evaluation milestones on Page 5 of the form.

### 12-Month Evaluation

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Who writes it** | Student (self-evaluation) |
| **Who reviews and signs** | Employer |
| **Due date** | Within 12 months of STEM OPT start date |
| **Submission deadline** | No later than 10 days after the 12-month mark |
| **Content** | Reflect on knowledge and skills acquired during the first year, progress toward learning objectives, any adjustments to training |

### 24-Month Final Evaluation

| Item | Detail |
|------|--------|
| **Who writes it** | Student (final self-evaluation) |
| **Who reviews and signs** | Employer |
| **Due date** | At the end of the STEM OPT extension period |
| **Submission deadline** | No later than 10 days after the conclusion of the training period |
| **Content** | Comprehensive recap of all training and knowledge acquired during the full 24-month period |

### Consequences of Missing Evaluations

Failure to submit either evaluation is a violation of the I-983 terms and may jeopardize the student's F-1 nonimmigrant status. The DSO is required to keep evaluations in the student's record.

## 7. Material Changes -- What Requires a New I-983

A new or modified I-983 must be filed whenever there is a **material change** to the training plan. Both the student and employer must sign the updated form, and it must be submitted to the DSO.

### Changes That Require a New I-983

| Change Type | Example |
|-------------|---------|
| **Employer change** | Student changes jobs entirely |
| **EIN change** | Corporate restructuring results in new tax ID |
| **Compensation reduction** | Pay decrease not tied to reduced hours |
| **Hours reduction** | Significant decrease in weekly hours |
| **Below minimum hours** | Drop below the 20-hour/week minimum |
| **Supervisor change** | New direct supervisor assigned |
| **Duties/role change** | Significant change in job responsibilities |
| **Learning objectives change** | Training focus shifts substantially |
| **Work location change** | Move to a different office or site |
| **Oversight structure change** | Changes to supervision or mentoring arrangements |

### Reporting Deadline

Material changes must be reported by submitting a modified I-983 to the DSO **at the earliest available opportunity** -- most schools interpret this as within **10 days** of the change.

### Signature Requirements

All signatures in Sections 2, 4, and 6 must be updated with new dates on the modified form to show all parties agree to the changes.

## 8. Common Mistakes in Filling Out the I-983

Based on university international office guidance and DSO feedback, these are the most frequent errors:

### Content Mistakes
1. **Vague Section 5**: The most common rejection reason. Listing job duties instead of learning objectives and STEM connections.
2. **No degree connection**: Failing to explain how the job relates to the specific STEM degree and CIP code.
3. **Generic supervision plan**: Writing "supervisor will oversee work" instead of describing specific mentoring structures.
4. **Missing compensation details**: Leaving salary/wage blank or providing a range instead of a specific figure.

### Administrative Mistakes
5. **Missing signatures**: All three signature sections (2, 4, 6) must be signed and dated. One missing signature = rejected form.
6. **Wrong EIN format**: Must be 9 digits in ##-####### format. Some employers provide the wrong number.
7. **Missing NAICS code**: Employers often do not know their NAICS code; students must look it up.
8. **Incorrect SEVIS ID or CIP code**: Transcription errors in student information fields.
9. **Outdated form version**: Using an old version of the I-983 instead of the current one from ICE.
10. **Pre-filling evaluation sections**: The 12-month and 24-month evaluation boxes should be left blank at initial submission.

### Strategic Mistakes
11. **Submitting too late**: Not leaving enough time for DSO review before the OPT expiration deadline.
12. **Not getting employer buy-in early**: Approaching the employer at the last minute leads to rushed, weak training plans.

## 9. What DSOs Look for When Reviewing I-983

DSOs are the gatekeepers. They must review the I-983 before recommending the STEM OPT extension in SEVIS.

### DSO Review Checklist

- **Completeness**: All fields filled, all signatures present and dated
- **STEM degree connection**: Training plan clearly explains how the work relates to the qualifying STEM degree
- **Specific learning goals**: Objectives identify specific knowledge, skills, or techniques -- not vague promises
- **Achievement plan**: Describes concrete methods for how goals will be reached
- **Supervision and oversight**: Names a supervisor, describes mentoring and evaluation structures
- **Commensurate terms**: Compensation and conditions match similarly situated U.S. workers
- **Employer attestations**: All required employer certifications are properly signed
- **E-Verify enrollment**: Employer must be enrolled in E-Verify (verified separately)

### What DSOs Do NOT Do

- DSOs are **not required** to conduct outside research into the employer
- DSOs are **not expected** to possess technical knowledge of STEM fields
- DSOs evaluate the form for compliance and completeness, not technical accuracy of the training content

### Grounds for Rejection

A DSO may decline to recommend a STEM OPT extension if:
- The employer has not provided required attestations
- The I-983 does not reflect compliance with reporting, evaluation, and other STEM OPT requirements
- The training plan is too vague to demonstrate a STEM connection

## 10. Tips for Getting Employer Cooperation

Many employers are unfamiliar with the I-983. Here is how to make the process smoother.

### Before the Conversation
- **Educate yourself first.** Understand every section so you can guide the employer.
- **Prepare a draft.** Fill in as much as you can (Section 1, draft learning objectives for Section 5) so the employer only needs to add their parts.
- **Bring the official instructions.** Share the [ICE I-983 Instructions PDF](https://www.ice.gov/doclib/sevis/pdf/i983Instructions.pdf) so the employer sees this is a government form, not a personal request.

### During the Conversation
- **Frame it as low-burden.** Emphasize that most of the training plan can reference existing onboarding, performance review, or mentoring programs.
- **Explain the "why."** The form exists to document that the job is a genuine training opportunity connected to the STEM degree. It protects the employer too.
- **Provide examples.** Share sample I-983s from your university's international office (most publish them online).
- **Identify the right signer.** The employer official must have "signatory authority" and be familiar with the student's goals and performance -- this is typically HR, a direct manager, or a department head.

### Common Employer Concerns and Responses

| Concern | Response |
|---------|----------|
| "Is this a sponsorship?" | No. STEM OPT is an extension of student status. No visa petition or legal fees required. |
| "Do we need a lawyer?" | No. The form is straightforward. It documents the training relationship that already exists. |
| "What is E-Verify?" | A free DHS system to verify employment eligibility. Enrollment is required for STEM OPT employers. |
| "What if we get audited?" | Compliance is simple: provide training as described, maintain commensurate wages, and cooperate with any site visits. |
| "We have never done this." | Many companies employ STEM OPT students. University international offices can help walk the employer through the process. |

## 11. Timeline for Submitting I-983

| When | Action | Deadline |
|------|--------|----------|
| **Before STEM OPT application** | Complete and sign I-983 with employer | Must be submitted to DSO before DSO can recommend STEM OPT in SEVIS |
| **STEM OPT application to USCIS** | File Form I-765 with USCIS | Up to 90 days before current OPT expires, no later than expiration date |
| **New job during STEM OPT** | Submit new I-983 for the new employer | Within **10 days** of starting new employment |
| **Material change occurs** | Submit modified I-983 | At earliest opportunity (within **10 days** of change) |
| **12-month mark** | Submit 12-month self-evaluation (Page 5, Box 1) | No later than **10 days** after the 12-month anniversary of STEM OPT start |
| **End of STEM OPT** | Submit final 24-month evaluation (Page 5, Box 2) | No later than **10 days** after the conclusion of STEM OPT |
| **Employer terminates student** | Employer reports to DSO | Within **5 business days** |

### Practical Advice on Timing

- Start the I-983 process **at least 4-6 weeks** before your OPT expiration to allow time for employer coordination, DSO review, and any revisions.
- Universities often have processing queues. Submit well before deadlines.
- Set calendar reminders for the 12-month evaluation -- missing it can jeopardize your status.
