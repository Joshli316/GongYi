# F-1 OPT Unemployment Day Counting Rules

> Comprehensive reference for understanding how unemployment days are tracked, counted, and enforced during Optional Practical Training (OPT) and STEM OPT extension periods.

---

## 1. The 90-Day Unemployment Limit (Post-Completion OPT)

Under federal regulations at **8 CFR 214.2(f)(10)(ii)(E)**, an F-1 student on post-completion OPT may not accumulate more than **90 days of aggregate unemployment** during their 12-month OPT authorization period.

Key facts:

- The 90-day clock starts on the **EAD start date** (the date printed on your Employment Authorization Document), not the date you receive the card or the date you first begin working.
- Every calendar day without qualifying employment counts -- including weekends and holidays.
- The count is **cumulative (aggregate)**, not consecutive. If you are unemployed for 30 days, then work for 2 months, then become unemployed again, those original 30 days still count toward your total.
- SEVIS tracks unemployment automatically by looking at employer records. Any day without an employer logged in SEVIS counts as an unemployment day.

## 2. The 150-Day Limit for STEM OPT

Students approved for the **24-month STEM OPT extension** receive an additional 60 days of allowable unemployment, for a combined total of **150 days** across the entire OPT period (initial 12-month post-completion OPT + 24-month STEM extension).

| OPT Type | Unemployment Limit | Period Covered |
|---|---|---|
| Post-completion OPT (standard) | 90 days | 12-month OPT period |
| STEM OPT extension | 150 days total | 12-month OPT + 24-month STEM extension combined |

Critical details:

- The 150 days is **not** a fresh 150-day allowance. It **includes** any unemployment days already accrued during the initial 12-month OPT period.
- If you used 45 unemployment days during your initial OPT, you have 105 days remaining for the entire STEM extension period (150 - 45 = 105).
- If you used all 90 days during your initial OPT, you only get 60 additional days during STEM OPT (150 - 90 = 60).
- The unemployment limit is not extended by periods of part-time employment or unemployment.

## 3. What Counts as "Unemployment"

This is where most confusion arises. Whether a day counts as "unemployment" depends on the **type of work**, **hours per week**, and **relationship to your field of study**.

### Employment That Stops the Unemployment Clock

To stop accruing unemployment days, your work must meet **all** of these criteria:

1. **At least 20 hours per week** (part-time minimum)
2. **Directly related to your major area of study**
3. **Reported to your DSO** and entered into SEVIS

The following types of qualifying employment are permitted during post-completion OPT:

| Employment Type | Stops Unemployment Clock? | Requirements |
|---|---|---|
| Full-time paid employment (20+ hrs/week) | Yes | Must be related to field of study |
| Part-time paid employment (20+ hrs/week) | Yes | Must be related to field of study |
| Part-time paid employment (<20 hrs/week) | **No** | Still accrues unemployment days |
| Multiple part-time jobs totaling 20+ hrs/week | Yes | Each must be related to field of study |
| Self-employment / freelance | Yes | 20+ hrs/week, related to field, proper business license |
| Unpaid internship / volunteering (20+ hrs/week) | Yes | Must be related to field; must not violate labor laws |
| Unpaid volunteering (<20 hrs/week) | **No** | Does not stop the clock |
| "Spirit-of-service" volunteering (church, hospital) | **No** | Not considered qualifying employment |
| Employment for a staffing/temp agency | Yes | Must be related to field of study, 20+ hrs/week |

### Self-Employment Rules

F-1 students on post-completion OPT **can** be self-employed, but must meet strict requirements:

- Work at least 20 hours per week
- The work must be directly related to your degree
- You must have proper business licenses (federal, state, local)
- You should be able to demonstrate active engagement (clients, projects, revenue)
- Report self-employment to your DSO within 10 days
- **Important**: Self-employment is allowed on initial post-completion OPT but is **not permitted** on STEM OPT unless you are working through your own employer that is enrolled in E-Verify

### Unpaid Volunteering Details

- **Field-related volunteering** at 20+ hours per week stops the clock, but it must be genuinely related to your major and must not violate wage/labor laws (e.g., you cannot "volunteer" at a for-profit company in a role that would normally be paid).
- **General volunteering** (at a place of worship, food bank, school) does not count as qualifying OPT employment and does **not** stop the unemployment clock.

### STEM OPT Additional Restrictions

During the STEM extension specifically:

- You must work for an employer enrolled in **E-Verify**
- Employment must be at least **20 hours per week**
- You must have a formal **Training Plan (Form I-983)** filed with your DSO
- You cannot be self-employed or work as an independent contractor during STEM OPT (unlike initial OPT)

## 4. How Days Are Counted: Cumulative, Not Consecutive

### The Aggregate Rule

The unemployment count is **cumulative (aggregate)**. This is a critical distinction:

- **Cumulative**: All individual days of unemployment throughout your OPT period are added together, regardless of gaps between them.
- It does **not** reset when you find a new job. Getting a job pauses the counter; losing it resumes counting from where it left off.

### What SEVIS Counts

SEVIS calculates unemployment by looking backward from the current date to the **Actual OPT Start Date** and counting every day that has no employer information on file. Since the SEVIS 6.41 update (October 2018), the system displays aggregate unemployment totals.

The unemployment counter in SEVIS shows:

| Field | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Days Allowed | Total unemployment days permitted (90 or 150) |
| Days Accrued | Total unemployment days counted so far |
| Days Remaining | Days Allowed minus Days Accrued |

### Weekends, Holidays, and Travel

- **Weekends**: Count as unemployment if you have no qualifying employment.
- **Holidays**: If you are employed, holidays during your employment period do **not** count as unemployment. If you have no employer, they count.
- **Travel outside the U.S.**: Days spent abroad while unemployed **still count** as unemployment days. The only exception is travel while employed (e.g., employer-authorized vacation or work-related travel).

## 5. What Happens When You Exceed the Limit

### Automatic SEVIS Termination

When a student exceeds the unemployment limit:

1. **SEVP sends a warning notice**: In recent enforcement actions (notably mid-2025), SEVP began sending letters to students whose SEVIS records show 90+ days without employer information, giving them **15 days** to update their records or face termination.
2. **SEVIS record termination**: SEVP will terminate the SEVIS record, typically within 15 days of the student reaching the unemployment threshold if no employer information is added.
3. **Loss of F-1 status**: Once SEVIS is terminated, the student is immediately out of F-1 status. There is no grace period.
4. **Employment authorization ends**: Your EAD is no longer valid once your SEVIS record is terminated.

### Consequences of Overstaying

| Consequence | Detail |
|---|---|
| Must leave the U.S. immediately | No 60-day grace period applies after termination |
| Unlawful presence begins accruing | Can trigger 3-year or 10-year reentry bars |
| Future visa applications jeopardized | May be denied based on status violation |
| Future immigration benefits affected | Can impact green card eligibility |
| Possible detention/removal | In severe cases |

### Options Before Hitting the Limit

If you are approaching the limit, you should act before reaching day 90 (or 150):

- **Find qualifying employment** (even part-time at 20+ hrs/week in your field)
- **Transfer to a new school** to pursue a new program
- **Change to another visa status** (e.g., B-1/B-2 tourist visa, though this has its own challenges)
- **Depart the U.S.** before exceeding the limit to preserve your immigration record

## 6. Special Rules for Pre-Completion OPT

Pre-completion OPT has fundamentally different unemployment rules:

- **No unemployment limit**: There is no 90-day unemployment restriction during pre-completion OPT. The unemployment counter does not apply.
- **However**, time authorized for pre-completion OPT is subtracted from the total 12 months of OPT available, whether or not you actually work during that period.
- Pre-completion OPT while school is in session is limited to **20 hours per week maximum**. During breaks, full-time work is permitted.
- Pre-completion OPT does not affect the 90-day unemployment counter that applies during post-completion OPT -- the counter only starts when post-completion OPT begins.

### Key Tradeoff

If you use 4 months of pre-completion OPT, you will only have 8 months of post-completion OPT remaining. But your 90-day unemployment allowance still applies to that shorter post-completion period.

## 7. How Unemployment Days Reset or Carry Over

### Within a Single OPT + STEM Period

- Unemployment days **carry over** from initial post-completion OPT into the STEM extension period. They are counted as one continuous total.
- The cap simply increases from 90 to 150 when STEM OPT is approved.
- Example: If you accrued 60 unemployment days during initial OPT and then receive STEM OPT, you have used 60 of your 150 allowed days (90 remaining).

### Cap-Gap Extensions

- If your OPT is automatically extended under the **H-1B cap-gap** provision (while an H-1B petition is pending), unemployment during the cap-gap period **still counts** toward the 90-day or 150-day limit.

### New Degree = Full Reset

- If you complete a **new degree at a higher level** and receive a new period of post-completion OPT, the unemployment counter **resets completely**.
- You get a fresh 90 days (or 150 if you also qualify for STEM OPT on the new degree).
- Regulations allow a maximum of **2 lifetime STEM extensions**, each requiring a new STEM-eligible degree at a higher educational level.

### Unemployment Day Reset Summary

| Scenario | Days Reset? |
|---|---|
| Getting a new job during current OPT | No -- counter pauses, does not reset |
| Transitioning from OPT to STEM OPT | No -- days carry over, cap increases to 150 |
| Cap-gap extension while awaiting H-1B | No -- days continue counting |
| New degree at higher level + new OPT | **Yes -- full reset to 90 (or 150)** |
| Leaving U.S. and returning | No -- days still count while abroad if unemployed |

## 8. Practical Counting Examples

### Example A: Straightforward Post-Completion OPT

> **Mia** graduates with a B.S. in Computer Science. Her EAD starts June 1. She starts her first job on July 15.

- June 1 -- July 14 = **44 days** of unemployment accrued
- July 15 onward = clock paused (employed)
- **Remaining**: 90 - 44 = **46 days** of unemployment left

### Example B: Job Gap During OPT

> **Raj** starts OPT on May 1 with a job. He is laid off on August 15 and finds a new job on October 1.

- May 1 -- August 15 = 0 unemployment days (employed)
- August 16 -- September 30 = **46 days** of unemployment
- October 1 onward = clock paused again
- **Remaining**: 90 - 46 = **44 days**

### Example C: Part-Time Work Below 20 Hours

> **Lin** starts OPT on June 1. She works 15 hours per week at a related job starting June 15.

- June 1 -- June 14 = 14 days of unemployment
- June 15 onward = **still accruing unemployment** because <20 hrs/week
- After 90 total days of unemployment, Lin is out of status -- even though she has been working
- **Lesson**: Work below 20 hours/week does NOT stop the clock

### Example D: STEM OPT Carry-Over

> **Wei** uses 70 unemployment days during his initial 12-month OPT. He then receives STEM OPT extension.

- Initial OPT unemployment: 70 days
- STEM OPT approved: cap increases from 90 to 150
- **Remaining**: 150 - 70 = **80 days** for the entire 24-month STEM extension
- If Wei has a 60-day gap between STEM jobs, he uses 60 more days: 150 - 130 = **20 days remaining** for the rest of the STEM period

### Example E: Travel While Unemployed

> **Soo** is on OPT but unemployed. She travels to Korea for 3 weeks (21 days).

- All 21 days abroad count as unemployment days
- There is no "pause" for international travel while unemployed
- If Soo had been employed and took a 3-week employer-approved vacation, those days would **not** count as unemployment

### Example F: Volunteering to Stop the Clock

> **Ahmed** graduates with a degree in Social Work. While job hunting on OPT, he volunteers 25 hours/week at a nonprofit doing case management (directly related to his degree).

- The volunteering qualifies as OPT employment because it is: (1) related to his field, (2) 20+ hrs/week, and (3) does not violate labor laws
- His unemployment clock is **paused** during this volunteer work
- He must report this to his DSO and have it entered in SEVIS

### Example G: Self-Employment on Initial OPT

> **Priya** has an M.S. in UX Design. She starts a freelance UX consultancy on her OPT start date.

- She registers an LLC, obtains a business license, and works 25+ hours/week
- She reports her self-employment to her DSO with documentation
- Her unemployment clock does **not** accrue days because she meets all requirements
- **If she later applies for STEM OPT**: she cannot continue as self-employed -- she must work for an E-Verify enrolled employer

## Quick Reference Summary

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| Post-completion OPT limit | 90 days cumulative unemployment |
| STEM OPT limit | 150 days cumulative (includes initial OPT days) |
| Pre-completion OPT limit | None (but time deducted from 12-month total) |
| Minimum work hours | 20 hours/week to stop the clock |
| Work must be | Related to major area of study |
| Self-employment | Allowed on initial OPT (not STEM OPT) |
| Weekends/holidays | Count as unemployment if no employer on file |
| Travel while unemployed | Counts as unemployment |
| Counter type | Cumulative / aggregate (never resets mid-period) |
| Full reset | Only with new degree at higher level + new OPT |
| Reporting deadline | 10 days to notify DSO of any employment change |
| Exceeding the limit | SEVIS termination, loss of F-1 status, must depart U.S. |
